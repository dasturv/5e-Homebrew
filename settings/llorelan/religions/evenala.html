Evenala

The Amber Queen, Heavenly Mother, the Radiant Sister. All are names for Evenala, the goddess of the Abellids and twin sister of Alowe. Evenala is a surprisingly active deity, much akin to her beloved Abellids. She strives always to improve the lives of her followers, teaching them to cooperate, be loyal to one another, build communities and fiercely defend those communities they have built. Unlike her sister though, Evenala welcomes outside species into the ranks of her followers. Through this openness followers of Evenala hope to one day unite the whole world in peaceful, energetic community.

Cosmology

The followers of Evenala know that their goddess is a young one. She did not create the world, but instead of being a mark against her, the followers of the Amber Queen find pride in just how much their goddess has done in her (relatively) short time. The Abellid empire stretched from the northern most point of the Westlands all the way to the Dreadmoor. To them, Evenala's power and wisdom is self evident, with nearly a continent worth's of devotees. It is believed that when a follower of Evenala dies, their spirit goes to her realm within the Unseen, known as Paradis, in order to join her and all the other souls of the devout. Whether or not fallen followers are allowed into the holy realm is a topic of great debate amongst Evenala's followers, with beliefs ranging from outright banishment and annihiliation to merely a time of contemplation before being allowed into the fold.

Followers

The most obvious and common followers of Evenala are of course the Abellids. As the direct creations of Evenala, her worship is nearly universal amongst their number. Beyond them, most Formids at least offer some praise towards Evenala (though full conversion away from Alowe is exceedingly rare). The priestesses of Evenala, however, have found Elytrans to be the easiest and most devout of converts, with their love of community, collaboration and close relationship with the Abellids making Evenala an easy transition. Evenala is the most widely worshipped divine in the Westlands, but is very rarely worshipped in the east.

Structure

The worship of Evenala is an organized and deeply ingrained affair. Serving as the patron goddess of the Abellid empire, her worship is tied directly to the greatest political power in the Westlands. Priestesses of Evenala serve as both spiritual guides and oftentimes secular leaders for the communities they serve. The worship of Evenala is oftentimes treated as a professional field, with the basics taught in public education and priestesses and political leaders recieving specialized training in institutions dedicated to instructing the proper worship of the goddess.

Factions

Though all those who worship Evenala worship the same goddess, there are many groups who focus on different aspects of her, with varying levels of recognition from the core of Evenala's followers.

The Avengists
Predominantly followed by the Piquor Abellids and those in the military, the Avengists offer the most war-like interpretation of Evenala accepted by her wider church. The followers of this tradition focus predominantly on Evenala's commands to protect their communities, seeing the destruction of those who would threaten Evenala and her followers as the holiest of tasks. To the Avengists, their goddess is a fierce guardian, and one who rewards proactive action against the foes of her followers.

The Olivists
Named after their founder Olivi, a Forsor Abellid, the Olivists preach a more lively and joyful aspect of Evenala. Primarily followed by the Forsor and many Elytrans, the Olivists potray Evenala as youthful, bright and mirthful. Focusing more heavily on Evenala's focus on love, community and generosity, they see Evenala as a fountain of abundance.

The Traditionalists
Despite the name, it is not really known what the original worship of Evenala looked like. Nonetheless, the Traditionalists views are the most widespread and accepted take on their goddess. Centered within the Greenwood and championed by the Judisor, the viewpoint has the religious clout of housing the physical form of their goddess within the capital of Amberhome. To the traditionalists, Evenala is a wise and just ruler, and is the model to which all queens and leaders must strive towards.

The Sisterhood
Marked as heretics and radicals, the Sisterhood is a subset of extremist Abellids who see Evenala not as a goddess, but the first of their kind. To them, the Abellid form is holy, and all non-Abellids are lesser at best, and threats at worst. This confrontational and supremecist mindset led to the Sisterhood being ousted from Evenala's church proper, but their ideals still linger both in secret societies and as fragmented beliefs within Abellid society.

Key Figures

Queen Esaloni
The original founder of the Abellid empire. Queen Esaloni united the fractured hives of the early Abellids through equal parts diplomacy and military might. A shrewd general and brilliant politician, she formed many of the institutions that still drive the Empire to the current day.

Queen Ariso
After the Fracturing (see below), it was only through the efforts and reforms of the Judisor Queen Ariso that the Abellid Empire was drawn back together. She pushed many religious and societal reforms, remaking the Abellid Empire and regaining Evenala's favor.

Queen Otoya
The current ruling Queen of the Abellid Empire, and High Priestess of Evenala. Queen Otoya is more contemplative and spiritual than her recent predecessors. Focused more on law reform and religious matters than those of military, she has proven a decisive figure amongst the Abellids. Her detractors claim she leaves the Empire vulnerable to their enemies to their south, though most Abellids see her as fixing many wrongs that have slipped by during the last century of warring.

Myths and Legends

Birth of the Twins
It is believed that the twin goddesses, Evenala and Alowe, were born in the far north, on the Plains of Origin. They were formed from the light of the first rays of the spring sun reflecting off the melting ice. There they wandered for twelve years, far from any life besides the beasts and plants. After this time they were filled with longing and loneliness, setting out south.

The Creation of the Abellids
After her wandering south with her sister, Evenala found the trees of the Greenwood. Enamored with their granduer, she climbed to their highest branches, where light and leaf meet. She made her home there, though she was still filled with loneliness. She saw how the beasts of the land created children, and without peer or mate, she took sap from the trees, and using the light of dawn and dusk, sculpted her daughters, the first of the Abellids.

Evenala's Ascension
After long years of living amongst her daughters, watching them grow, raise children, and eventually die, Evenala grew weary again. Missing her lost daughters, she sealed her form in amber, leaving her physical body to find her children within the realm of spirits. She left with a promise: to be there for all her daughters and children when they crossed to the other side, just as they would be there for one another on Llorelan.

The Fracturing
It was during the time of Queen Ussulti when the Abellids, straying from their goddess, turned on one another in a fervent religious divide. Unable to agree upon matters of worship nor state, the Empire shattered into three. Heartbroken at her daughter's warring and unable to pick any of her children over another, Evenala turned from the world. At the departure of their goddess, the Abellids were thrown into despair.
