Noxella

Noxella, goddess of the moon, the Silver Lady and second of the Radiant Trio. From her realm within Celestia she promotes gentle kindness, grace, empathy and resilience in the face of evil. She reaches out to aid the suffering and meek, and teaches her devout to do the same. Noxella does not care what race, age, upbringing or past her followers have, for all have the capacity for good in her name.

Cosmology

Noxella is old, and while she did not create the world, she has seen ages come and pass. To her followers, she is a timeless force of goodness, tirelessly working to save a broken world. It is believed that when a follower of Noxella, or even just a good bug not devoted to another god, dies, their soul goes to her moonlit realm in Celestia: a land of quiet song and tranquil silver streams. There the dead devout enjoy blissful peace forevermore.

Followers

Noxella and the rest of the Radiant Trio are not exclusive to any race, culture or region. Instead, their church spreads to wherever their message would take root. For Noxella, this means her strongest centers of worship lie within the Whistlewood amongst the Cuinin Amhrani and throughout the Abellid empire, with an exceptionally strong concentration in the Forsor's territory. Despite this, she is worshipped everywhere throughout Llorelan, from grand cathedrals to private shrines in her devout's homes.

Structure

Noxella's doctrine is predominantly spread through her church, where formalized education, priests, and bylaws hold sway. That being said, the church of Noxella is not afraid of sects popping up where her message is needed. This has led to Noxella's worship becoming a strange blend of the other two of the Radiant Trio, with small sects like Stellis and organized religion like Solaris. Though, while this hybrid approach has led to a lot of flexibility, it also provides tension between the more and less organized portions of her worshippers.

Factions

Unionists
Made up primarily by the central church of Noxella, the Unionists believe that while the sects of Noxella worship are good, they should aim to become part of the church structure over time. Citing fears over disunity, doctrine shift and even outright blasphemy, the Unionists work tirelessly to find and incorporate the disparate groups of Noxella's worshippers.

Fragment Sects
While not known by any specific name, many of the individual sects of Noxella worship resist become absorbed into the greater structure of Noxella's church. Oftentimes these sects form in places of great suffering, growing a following based on the tenets of Noxella that best fits the needs of the community. When the wider church steps in, many of these traits that made Noxella so appealing to those followers are stripped away or pushed from the forefront, oftentimes leading to resentment and even collapse.

Myths and Legends

Noxella's Ascension
It is said that Noxella was born within Celestial as a great silver moth. She grew and learned from within the blessed realm for ages until one day she found a window into Llorelan: the moon. She gazed down onto the bugs of the physical world, toiling and laughing and living. And yet, she also saw struggle, pain and suffering. Moved by the lives of the mortal bugs she entered the world, seeking to ease the pain. And yet, despite all she could do, she could not be everywhere at once. So Noxella returned to her home and ascended, her mind reaching out over all of Llorelan. Now it is her followers who help those who struggle, with Noxella's patient gaze watching over all.

The Moonlit Dew
During elder days, when the first Amhrani came to the Whistlewood, there was a young singer named Cualthaine. Young Cualthaine was alone, his family dead, friends gone and heart heavy. He wandered through the woods singing his grief until his throat was raw and voice gone. He fell to his knees and wept, but as he did he found droplets of dew around him, the moon reflecting within their form. He drank from the dew and felt his throat begin to soothe, and with it his heart as well. He heard a voice: "I am Noxella, and I have heard your grief. I offer you the dew of Celestia to ease your burdens and focus your song. Go into the world, young Cualthaine, and continue to sing. Bring ease to those who suffer."

Saint Gattalix
Gattalix was a Doraithe, and quite a vile one at that. He was the leader of a band of slavers. He and his followers broke many families, sold innocents and abused many. He had wealth, power, and all the vices he wanted. And yet, when he captured a priest of Noxella, he was shaken. The priest's words baffled and enchanted him, and his curiosity grew. Over time Noxella's teachings took hold and his heart grew heavy with the guilt he had accrued over his life. He left his band, travelling and living humbly, giving back in any way he could. In the end he died fighting to protect a family from a roving band of Doraithe, a sacrifice that cemented his sainthood.

Tenets

Noxella teaches her to follow these tenets:

  • Do good with everything you do. The world is often a dark place, bring light to it in every action.
  • Relieve suffering. The anguish of the bugs of Llorelan needs to be stopped, be the balm of mercy and kindness to those who need it.
  • Show grace to those around you. Every bug in Llorelan has done bad things, be able to forgive and let others into the fold.
  • Do not let hope die. The fight we fight seems endless, but never let your flame die out.
Taboos

There are acts, while not unforgiveable, are seen as undeniably evil in the eyes of Noxella:

  • Never inflict senseless suffering on others. We are to end suffering, not be the source of it.
  • Never stand in the way of kindness. No matter your feelings, do not prevent others from doing the good work.
  • Never treat your voice as Noxella's. The Silver Lady can speak to those she needs to, we are to be humble servants.

Impacts on Society

Noxella's church being so widespread and ancient has made many of her tenets permeate beyond her temples. Many nation's support for the poor, refugees and even medicine are at least partially inspired by the work of Noxella's followers. Additionally, when combined with the rest of the Radiant Trio, Noxella's tenets serve as a near universal measure of what it means to be "good".

Symbolism and Practices

With how long Noxella's church has had to develop traditions, it is no surprise they have a rich history in rituals and symbolism. Imagery of the moon, dewdrops, moths and other gentle motifs of night are common, with silver, whites and soft blues being everywhere. Noxellan rituals tend to be done at night and are steeped in silence or quiet song, with a thick air of reverence.
