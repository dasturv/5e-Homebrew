Philosophy: Ko'oshibotsu

Ko'oshibotsu

Translating roughly to "score settling" or "grudge balancing", Ko'oshibotsu is a philosophy built on resolving one's debts before death, whether that be their own or another's passing. Followers of Ko'oshibotsu leave no slight unpunished, but also no debt left unpaid.

Cosmology

While different followers of Ko'oshibotsu have different ideas on the afterlife, they agree that grievances not only linger after death, but fossilize. Once a soul passes across the veil, it can no longer settle grudges, leading to a permanent anger or sorrow. As time passes the added weight and severity of these unresolved grudges manifest in the physical world as natural disasters: storms, plagues, floods and fallow. Ko'oshibotsu teaches that only the living can settle debts, and must settle their own and aim to resolve those of the dead when possible.

Grudges and Debts

What is considered a viable grudge or debt does have some variance, but generally they must aid or harm one's ability to survive or their reputation. For example, stealing food from someone, physically harming someone or lying about one's own skills would all lead to grudges. A grudge can be resolved by either the victim hurting the offender's chance of survival or the offender giving something up to the victim of equal value of what was lost. Generally, grudges can be resolved personally, but there are cultural figures who oversee contentious or important grudges, as well as the dead.

There are also several factors that can make a grudge invalid. Actions committed to prevent a grudge cannot be used as a grudge (provided the action did not exceed the prevented grudge), such as defending oneself or one's territory. Furthermore, actions committed as part of a sworn agreement cannot be used as a grudge, though failure to do such actions can. Finally, things gained or lost due to nature or fate cannot be used as a grudge, such as losing a house in a storm or treasure in a game of chance.

Followers

Ko'oshibotsu is deeply ingrained in the cultures of the Savage Lands, specifically the Eqrab, Harish and Scara (the native Elytrans). It is relatively rare to see outside of this region, but within the Savage Lands themselves, Ko'oshibotsu is the rule of the land.

Structure

While the individual rules and cultures differ, each of the cultures that follow Ko'oshibotsu have central figures that serves in a position of power and manages the debts of the living and the dead. Amongst the Eqrab there are the Zo'bi'o (priests) and Bonbotsu (balancers), the Scara have the Rayiys (chiefs), and the Harish have the Kalushabi (scribes). The philosophy encapsulates the entirety of the people's lives, showing up in every level.

Factions

Lushabi
Meaning "written word" in the Harish language and predominantly followed by them, Lushabi is a far more rigid and communal form of Ko'oshibotsu. Gaining its name from the stone structure found in the heart of Harish settlements, followers of Lushabi swear oaths to uphold the honor and rules of their communities, with the laws literally set in stone. These oaths expands their grudges to their whole community, making a slight against one a slight against all, and breaking the laws being seen as a grudge for the whole community to resolve. Furthermore, followers of Lushabi see grudges as things to avoid when possible, seeing them as more sinful than other factions of Ko'oshibotsu may.

Shibeti
Translating roughly to "living wisely" in the Eqrab language, Shibeti is a looser and more individualistic form of Ko'oshibotsu. Followers of Shibeti settle grudges on an individual basis, with no additional rules or laws expanding on the core ideas of Ko'oshibotsu. They see grudges as facts of life that must be resolved, but also unavoidable in such a cruel world.
