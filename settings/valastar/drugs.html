Pixie Dust

In a world where so many seek an escape, either from horrible living conditions or intense boredom in a cushioned life, drugs are all too common. Ranging from benign party drugs to life threatening combat stims, drugs in valastar serve a very similar purpose as potions in other worlds, though with far more dangerous side effects.

Obtaining Drugs
Drugs are spread across the entire breadth of legality, cost and rarity. The more illegal and more rare a drug, the harder it is to find and purchase. Legal drugs can be found in general stores, pharmacies and factory canteens throughout the city. Restricted drugs are more specialized, being sold by doctors and pharmacists and often require some form of prescription or registration to purchase and sell. Illegal drugs are only sold on black markets and through criminal contacts, and even being caught holding onto them can put you behind bars.

For example, lets say our poor little wiz-ganger Georgie was heading to a fight with a big and scary ogre, and decided he needed himself a boost. He tracked down his friend who sold pixie dust, an illegal but powerful magical drug. Expensive stuff, but after some haggling he managed to get some for the low price of fifty gelt and a future favor...

Doing Drugs
In order to gain the benefits of a drug, you need to introduce it to your system. Each drug has one or more Vectors, or methods to use it. These vectors can be contact, ingestion, inhalation, or injection. Each vector also has an Acting Time, or amount of time must pass after the vector method is used before the effects of the drug starts. After the acting time, a drug grants its effects. Finally, once the effects of the drug wears off, the user must make an addiction saving throw, as detailed below.

Georgie made sure to pack his pixie dust before heading out, dipping into an alleyway to inhale a pinch before stepping foot into his foe's territory. Being new to the stuff, he sneezes loudly, blowing iridescent powder everywhere. He thinks he did it right though, so he makes his way down the street. After a minute he sees his opponent, shoving some poor worker around, right as the drug's arcane power begins to course through him. He grins, this was gonna be easy...

Addiction
Once a drug's effects have worn off, a user must make a saving throw to prevent addiction, with the specific saving throw based on the drugs addictiveness and addiction save. The DC of this saving throw increases by 1 for each time the user had used the drug in the drug's addiction interval. If a user fails this saving throw, the become addicted to the drug. Whenever an addicted creature completes a long rest without using the addicted drug in the past 24 hours, the suffer the drug's withdrawal symptoms until the use the drug again.

The scrap goes well, and Georgie feels pretty good, maybe even too good. As his high fades and the magical effects of the drugs wears off, he needs to resist getting addicted. He had never done pixie dust before, much less previously this month, so the save DC for the drug is its base of 20 and targets Georgie's Wisdom. All said and done, Georgie has a +4 to Wisdom saves, and after rolling a respectable 15 on his save he has a total of 19! Just barely missing the DC, he becomes addicted. He slips back home, thinking how useful pixie dust is...

Escaping Addiction
Once a creature is addicted to a drug, it can be difficult escape that cycle. An creature addicted to a drug can repeat the addiction saving throw once each time the addicted drug's interval elapses. The DC of this saving still increases by 1 for each time the creature had used the drug during that interval.

Pixie dust was tearing Georgie's life apart. The expenses, the exhaustion, and worst of all the pain caused by spellcasting. His friends had an intervention over two months ago, and he was trying really hard to get clean. He wasn't perfect, he did use pixie dust three times last month, but he was doing better. At the end of the month he makes another saving throw against the addiction. This time the DC is 23 (+1 for each time he used pixie dust over the past month). With his friends help though, and some more experience under his belt, Georgie has advantage on the saving throw and has a +6 bonus this time. After rolling a 19, for a total of 25, he is finally able to put his addiction behind him...

Mixing Drugs
Having many drugs in your system at one time is a recipe for disaster. Whenever you use a drug while under the effects of another (or within one hour of using a drug with an instant effect), you must make a constitution saving throw. The DC for this saving throw is equal to the highest base addiction save DC amongst the mixed drugs, plus 2 for each other drug mixed. On a failure you gain one level of exhaustion. Taking multiple of the same drug still counts mixing drugs.

NuVita and Vitalophran

Unlike other worlds, Valastar does not have healing potions. Instead, they are replaced with a drug called NuVita as well as its related medicines.

NuVita

Common legal drug
Cost: 15gp
Vector(s): Injection (immediate)
Addictiveness: Negligible
Addiction Save: Constitution
Effects: This miracle drug comes in a large, bright green syringe. You recover 2d4+2 hit points.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to recover without the aid of NuVita. Whenever you would recover hit points from a source other than NuVita, you recover 1 less hit points (minimum of 1).

NuVita (Extra Strength)

Uncommon legal drug
Cost: 75gp
Vector(s): Injection (immediate)
Addictiveness: Low
Addiction Save: Constitution
Effects: This drug is a more potent version of classic NuVita. You recover 4d4+4 hit points.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to recover without the aid of NuVita. Whenever you would recover hit points from a source other than NuVita, you recover 2 less hit points (minimum of 1).

Vitalophran

Rare restricted drug
Cost: 250gp
Vector(s): Injection (immediate)
Addictiveness: Medium
Addiction Save: Constitution
Effects: This drug is an even stronger form of NuVita, made for security and other dangerous professions. You recover 8d4+8 hit points.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to recover without the aid of Vitalophran. Whenever you would recover hit points from a source other than Vitalophran, you recover 3 less hit points (minimum of 1).

Vitalophran (Concentrated)

Very rare restricted drug
Cost: 1,000gp
Vector(s): Injection (immediate)
Addictiveness: High
Addiction Save: Constitution
Effects: This drug is a concentrated form of Vitalophran (and to a lesser extent, NuVita). You recover 10d4+20 hit points.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to recover without the aid of Vitalophran. Whenever you would recover hit points from a source other than Vitalophran, you recover 4 less hit points (minimum of 1).

Vitalos Drakan

Legendary restricted drug
Cost: 5,000gp
Vector(s): Injection (immediate)
Addictiveness: Absurd
Addiction Save: Constitution
Effects: This drug is the active ingredient of Vitalophran and NuVita, discovered and purified by the dragonlord Nu Dende. You recover 16d4+30 hit points.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to recover without the aid of Vitalos Drakan. Whenever you would recover hit points from a source other than Vitalos Drakan, you recover 5 less hit points (minimum of 1).

Mana and Manathilyte

Just as healing potions have been replaced, so too has mana potions. More restricted than their health counterparts, the over the counter drug called Mana and its related drugs are considered something like a caffiene pill for wage-wizards.

Mana

Common restricted drug
Cost: 25gp
Vector(s): Ingestion (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: Low
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This magical drug comes in the form of small, bright blue tablets. You recover 1d2 levels of expended spell slots. None of the recovered slots can be of 6th level or higher. If you do not have a spell slot of 1st level or higher (expended or not), you take force damage equal to three times the total of your roll.
Alternatively, if you are using the Spell Points variant rule, you can instead recover 1d2+1 spent spell points. When you take force damage from this drug while using this variant rule, you take twice the total rolled as force damage instead of three times the total.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to cast spells without the help of mana. Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, roll a d20. On a 1, the spell fails and has no effect. The spell slot is still expended.

Mana (Extra Strength)

Uncommon restricted drug
Cost: 100gp
Vector(s): Ingestion (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: Medium
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This magical drug is a more potent form of Mana. You recover 2d2 levels of expended spell slots. None of the recovered slots can be of 6th level or higher. If you do not have a spell slot of 2nd level or higher (expended or not), you take force damage equal to three times the total of your roll.
Alternatively, if you are using the Spell Points variant rule, you can instead recover 2d2+2 spent spell points. When you take force damage from this drug while using this variant rule, you take twice the total rolled as force damage instead of three times the total.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to cast spells without the help of mana. Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, roll a d20. On a 2 or lower, the spell fails and has no effect. The spell slot is still expended.

Manathilyte

Rare illegal drug
Cost: 350gp
Vector(s): Ingestion (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: High
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This magical drug is the active ingredient in Mana, and is illegal for non-research purposes. You recover 2d2+2 levels of expended spell slots. None of the recovered slots can be of 6th level or higher. If you do not have a spell slot of 3rd level or higher (expended or not), you take force damage equal to three times the total of your roll.
Alternatively, if you are using the Spell Points variant rule, you can instead recover 3d2+3 spent spell points. When you take force damage from this drug while using this variant rule, you take twice the total rolled as force damage instead of three times the total.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to cast spells without the help of manathilyte. Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, roll a d20. On a 3 or lower, the spell fails and has no effect. The spell slot is still expended.

Concentrated Manathilyte

Very rare illegal drug
Cost: 1,250gp
Vector(s): Ingestion (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: High
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This magical drug is even more concentrated, and is rarely seen outside specific arcane research labs. You recover 2d2+4 levels of expended spell slots. None of the recovered slots can be of 6th level or higher. If you do not have a spell slot of 6th level or higher (expended or not), you take force damage equal to three times the total of your roll.
Alternatively, if you are using the Spell Points variant rule, you can instead recover 4d2+4 spent spell points. When you take force damage from this drug while using this variant rule, you take twice the total rolled as force damage instead of three times the total.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to cast spells without the help of manathylite. Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, roll a d20. On a 4 or lower, the spell fails and has no effect. The spell slot is still expended.

Aqua Magi

Legendary illegal drug
Cost: 7,500gp
Vector(s): Ingestion (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: Absurd
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This legendary liquid is pure manathilyte mixed with a dozen other magical compounds. You recover 3d2+4 levels of expended spell slots. None of the recovered slots can be of 6th level or higher. If you do not have a spell slot of 1st level or higher (expended or not), you take force damage equal to three times the total of your roll.
Alternatively, if you are using the Spell Points variant rule, you can instead recover 5d2+5 spent spell points. When you take force damage from this drug while using this variant rule, you take twice the total rolled as force damage instead of three times the total.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body struggles to cast spells without the help of Aqua Magi. Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, roll a d20. On a 5 or lower, the spell fails and has no effect. The spell slot is still expended.

Drugs

Below are the assorted drugs and medicines that can be procured and used in Valastar.

Fae Leaf

Uncommon illegal drug
Cost: 100gp
Vector(s): Inhalation (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: Low
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This magical leaf is rolled up and smoked. For the next hour you have advantage on saving throws against spells and magical effects.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body becomes more susceptible to magic. You have disadvantage on saving throws against spells and magical effects.
Special: Elves and other creatures with the Fey Ancestry trait have advantage on this drug's addiction saving throw.

Focus

Uncommon restricted drug
Cost: 75gp
Vector(s): Ingestion (30 minutes)
Addictiveness: Medium
Addiction Save: Intelligence
Effects: This drug takes the form of a small white tablet with an eye printed on it. For the next four hours you have advantage on saving throws to maintain concentration on spells or psionic practices.
Withdrawal Effects: You struggle to keep your mind on track. At the end of each of your turns, if you are concentrating on a spell or psionic practice, you must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or lose concentration.

Mandragora

Rare illegal drug
Cost: 750gp
Vector(s): Inhalation (1 minute)
Addictiveness: High
Addiction Save: Charisma
Effects: This strange mushroom when burnt and inhaled connects the user deeply with the natural world. For the next hour your Wisdom score changes to 27. This drug has no effect on you if your Wisdom is equal to or greater than 27.
Withdrawal Effects: You lose yourself in the depths of the cosmos, suffering ego death. Your Charisma score changes to 5.

Pixie Dust

Rare illegal drug
Cost: 250gp
Vector(s): Inhalation (1 minute)
Addictiveness: High
Addiction Save: Wisdom
Effects: This magical drug comes in the form of an iridescent pale powder. For the next hour, whenever you would cast a spell of 1st level or higher, treat that spell as if it was cast with a spell slot 1 level higher. This does not expend a higher level spell slot.
Withdrawal Effects: Your body is scorched by magical power. Whenever you would cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you take force damage equal to the level of that spell. This damage cannot be reduced in any way.
