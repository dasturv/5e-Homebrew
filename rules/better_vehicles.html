Better Vehicles

In standard 5e DnD, vehicles tend to be more for flavor than an actual important decision for a party. Using these variant rules, players have more in depth rules for vehicles as well as aility to customize and personalize their vehicles.

Inspiration: Shadowrun.

Vehicles

All vehicles have a list of attributes and characteristics unique to them, as detailed below:

Armor Class, Hit Points and Damage Thresholds

Every vehicle has an armor class and hit points that function the same as it does for creatures. A vehicle that is reduced to 0 hit points is destroyed. Some larger vehicles have multiple sets of armor class and hit points, each representing a smaller portion of the vehicle. If a part is reduced to 0 hit points and destroyed, it may have additional effects. If a vehicle has a damage threshold, it has immunity to any individual source of damage below that threshold.

Size

Each vehicle takes up a specified amount of space. The measurement is in the format of length x width x height.

Crew

Every vehicle has a minimum number of creatures required to control and steer it. Some vehicles may continue to move while not crewed, such as a cart pulled by a mule or a sailing ship in the wind. If a vehicle has less than the minimum number of crew members, then any check to steer or control the vehicle is made with disadvantage.

Movement Speed and Maneuverability

All vehicles have at least one speed, and many have multiple, each requiring specific requirements to obtain. Additionally, each vehicle has a maneuverability score, which represents the number of rounds it takes a vehicle to turn 90 degrees at full speed.

Cargo

Each vehicle has a maximum amount of cargo it can carry, measured in volume.

Land Vehicles

Here are the options for land vehicles:

Cart (15 gp)

Carts are small wooden vehicles designed to haul cargo and lacks any roofing. Oftentimes lightweight and cheap, they are truly utility first.

Cart
Land Vehicle (10x5x5 ft.)
Minimum Crew: 1
Speed: Same as Puller (see below)
Maneuverability: 2
Cargo: 200 square feet
Weight: 200 lbs
Defenses
Body Wheels
Armor Class: 15 Armor Class: 17
Hit Points: 30 Hit Points: 20
Features
Pulled: A pulled vehicle inherits the speed of of whatever creature or creatures are pulling it. Any cargo within the vehicle (including the weight of the vehicle itself) are considered to be five times lighter for the purposes of calculating the pulling creature's encumbrance.
Wheeled: A wheeled vehicle cannot turn if it is not moving forwards or backwards. Additionally, if the wheels of the vehicle are destroyed, it no longer reduces the weight of its cargo or itself for the purposes of calculating a pulling creature's encumbrance.
Sled (20 gp)

Sleds are small wooden vehicles designed to haul cargo and people across frozen ground and snow. Sleds are useless on other types of ground.
