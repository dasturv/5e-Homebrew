Variant Rule: Contacts

In standard 5e DnD, characters from player's backstories tend to be rather, unimpactful outside of key story moments. This variant rule aims to remedy this by adding a new type of npc: contacts.

Inspiration: Shadowrun.

What is a Contact

A contact is someone that the player character knows and that can provide useful services or influence. Not every person a player has met is a contact, and contacts are not added after character creation (instead, use the normal rules for social interaction). Contacts are solely to flesh out and provide benefits for the player's past connections.

Creating Contacts

Upon character creation, players gain a number of Contact Points (CP) to spend on creating contacts. a player gains a number of these points equal to the Charisma score. Each contact has an influence and a loyalty score, and their cost in CP increases with these two scores, as shown on the tables below. A contact's total cost cannot exceed half of the players Charisma score (rounded down).

Contact Influence Scores
Score Influence Description Cost
0 No Influence This character is only useful for their own skills and knowledge. Average citizens fall in this category. 1
1 Local Influence This character is a respected leader of a community. Mayors, local guild leaders and minor nobility fall in this category. 3
2 Wide Influence This character has influence across a wide section of the world. National leaders, high priestesses of major gods and famous adventurers fall into this category. 5
3 Global Influence This character influences the entire world, and possibly others as well. This category includes immortal beings such as gods, demon lords and elemental princes. 7
Contact Loyalty Scores
Score Influence Description Cost
0 Aquaintance This character holds no strong feelings towards the player. They are counted as indifferent to start (attitude score 0). 1
1 Friend This character likes and respects the player. They are counted as friendly to start (attitude score 10). 3
2 Beloved This character loves the player and does what they can to help them. They are counted as beloved to start (attitude score 30). 5
3 Idol This character sees the player as a savior and idolizes them, trusting them wholly. They are counted as idolized to start (attitude score 55). 7

Interacting with Contacts

A contact is just like any other character. They have their own wants, needs and goals, with contacts of higher influence being harder to get a hold of and having more complicated needs. If a player betrays the trust given to them, or aids the contact in some way, their attitude score increases and decreases just as any other npc. Failure to maintain a contact can lead them to slip away, losing the benefits they provided.
