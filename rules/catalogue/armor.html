Catalogue: Armor

Armor (and shields) are the primary defense of most adventurers, but being well protected comes at great cost both in gold and weight.

Light, Medium and Heavy
Armors are seperated into light, medium and heavy. Classes and features provide proficiency with armors and shields. To use a shield, you must be proficient with shields and the corresponding armor category. While using a set of armor or shield you are not proficient in, or do not meet the armor's strength requirement, you still gain its AC bonus, but you have disadvantage on any ability check, saving throw, or Attack roll that involves Strength or Dexterity, and you canâ€™t cast Spells.

Donning and Doffing
Putting on and taking off armor isn't instantaneous. The table below shows how long it takes to don (put on) or doff (take off) your armor.

Resting in Armor
Sleeping in armor is uncomfortable. If you take a long rest while wearing a set of armor you do not reduce your exhaustion level and gain one level of exhaustion. However, you do not need to take off all of your armor to rest, only the hard plates and rigid sections, leaving the flexible layers underneath. While wearing this reduced armor, your AC is reduced to the value in the table below. To get into or out of your reduced armor takes half of the respective don/doff time.
