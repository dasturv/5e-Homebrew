Adventurers need a lot more than just their weapons and armor. Food, bedding, shelter, entertainment, tools and more are all important things to bring when leaving the comforts of civilization. Below are several tables of items players can purchase to aid them throughout an adventure.

Equipment Packs

In addition to the items below, there are things called equipment packs. Equipment packs offer many items in a bundle and have a reduced cost. Players can usually only purchase equipment packs upon character creation, though DMs may choose to have them offered later by merchants as a special deal or rewards.

Adventuring Gear
Item Cost Weight Description
Acid (vial) 15 gp 1/4 lb. As an action, you can splash the contents of this vial onto a creature within 5 feet of you or throw the vial up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. In either case, make a ranged attack against a creature or object, treating the acid as an improvised weapon. On a hit, the target takes 2d6 acid damage.
Alchemist's Fire (flask) 20 gp 1/2 lb. This sticky, adhesive fluid ignites when exposed to air. As an action, you can throw this flask up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. Make a ranged attack against a creature or object, treating the alchemist's fire as an improvised weapon. On a hit, the target takes 1d4 fire damage at the start of each of its turns. A creature can end this damage by using its action to make a DC 10 Dexterity check to extinguish the flames.
Antitoxin (vial) 25 gp 1/4 lb. A creature that drinks this vial of liquid gains advantage on saving throws against poison for 1 hour. It confers no benefits to undead or constructs.
Ball Bearings (bag of 1,000) 1 gp 2 lb. As an action, you can spill these tiny balls from their pouch to cover a level area 10 feet square. A creature moving across the covered area must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw or fall prone. A creature moving through the area at half speed doesn't need to make the saving throw.
Bedroll 1 gp 2 lb. A thick and cushioned blanket made to wrap around you and seal on one side. A bedroll contributes towards a poor lifestyle.
Bell 1 gp - A small bell. Can be clearly heard from 30 feet away when rung.
Blanket 5 sp 1 lb. A simple square blanket to help keep the chill out. A blanket contributes towards a squalid lifestyle.
Block and Tackle 1 gp 5 lb. A set of pulleys with a cable threaded through them and a hook to attach to objects, a block and tackle allows you to hoist up to four times the weight you can normally lift.
Book 25 gp 5 lb. A book may contain poetry, historical accounts, information on a particular field of lore, diagrams of gnomish contraptions or just about anything else that can be represented in text or pictures. A book can be consulted for at least one minute to grant a d4 bonus to a single ability check made within the next hour, provided the check is relating to the book's contents. Once a creature has used a book in this way three times, that creature can no longer gain the bonus from the book.
Caltrops (bag of 20) 1 gp 2 lb. As an action, you can spread a single bag of caltrops to cover a 5-foot-square area. Any creature that enters the area must succeed on a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or stop moving and take 1 piercing damage. Until the creature regains at least 1 hit point, its walking speed is reduced by 10 feet. A creature moving through the area at half speed doesn't need to make the saving throw.
Candle 1 cp 1/4 lb. For 1 hour a candle sheds pright light in a 5-foot radius and dim light for another 5 feet.
Censer 1 gp 5 lb. A metal bowl or vessel designed to hold burning incense. Can be held via a chain.

Healer's Kit 15 gp 3 lb. This kit is a leather pouch containing bandages, salves, and splints. The kit has five uses. As an action, you can expend one use of the kit to stabilize a creature that has 0 hit points, without needing to make a Wisdom (Medicine) check. Alternatively, you can expend one use of the kit and treat a creature's injuries for one minute. At the end of the minute that creature can spend and roll up to one of its unspent hit dice, recovering hit points equal to the roll plus their Constitution modifier.
Holy Water (flask) 15 gp 1/2 lb. As an action, you can splash the contents of this flask onto a creature within 5 feet of you or throw it up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. In either case, make a ranged attack against a target creature, treating the holy water as an improvised weapon. If the target is a fiend or undead, it takes 2d6 radiant damage.
Hourglass 25 gp 1 lb. A device made to accurately track a set amount of time. Usually 1 hour.
Hunting Trap 5 gp 25 lb. When you use your action to set it, this trap forms a saw-toothed steel ring that snaps shut when a creature steps on a pressure plate in the center. The trap is affixed by a heavy chain to an immobile object, such as a tree or a spike driven into the ground. A creature that steps on the plate must succeed on a DC 13 Dexterity saving throw or take 1d4 piercing damage and stop moving. Thereafter, until the creature breaks free of the trap, its movement is limited by the length of chain (typically 3 feet long). A creature can use its action to make a DC 13 Strength check, freeing itself or another creature within its reach on a success. Each failed check deals 1 piercing damage to the trapped creature.
Incense 5 gp - A block or set of incense sticks. When ignited it burns for 1 hour, during which it exudes a pleasant scent. When a creature within 10 feet of the burning incense makes an Intelligence, Wisdom or Charisma saving throw, they may add a d4 to the total. Once a creature has gained this benefit, they cannot gain it again from any incense for 24 hours.
Ink (1 oz) 1 gp - A small vial of black ink. 1 oz of ink can be used to write 500 pages worth of text or 50 pages of illustrations.

Oil (flask) 1 sp 1/2 lb. Oil usually comes in a clay flask that holds 1 pint. As an action, you can splash the oil in this flask onto a creature within 5 feet of you or throw it up to 20 feet, shattering it on impact. Make a ranged attack against a target creature or object, treating the oil as an improvised weapon. On a hit, the target is covered in oil. If the target takes any fire damage before the oil dries (after 1 minute), the target takes an additional 5 fire damage from the burning oil. You can also pour a flask of oil on the ground to cover a 5-foot-square area, provided that the surface is level. If lit, the oil burns for 2 rounds and deals 5 fire damage to any creature that enters the area or ends its turn in the area. A creature can take this damage only once per turn.

Spellcasting Supplies
Item Cost Weight Description
Arcane Focus 10 gp 1 lb. An arcane focus is a special item — an orb, a crystal, a rod, a specifically constructed staff, a wand-like length of wood, or some similar item — designed to channel the power of arcane spells. A sorcerer, warlock, or wizard can use such an item as a spellcasting focus.
Component Pouch 5 gp 2 lb. A component pouch is a small, watertight leather belt pouch that has compartments to hold all the material components and other special items you need to cast your spells, except for those components that have a specific cost (as indicated in a spell's description).
Druidic Focus 1 gp 1 lb. A druidic focus might be a sprig of mistletoe or holly, a wand or scepter made of yew or another special wood, a staff drawn whole out of a living tree, or a totem object incorporating feathers, fur, bones, and teeth from sacred animals. A druid can use such an object as a spellcasting focus.
Holy Symbol 5 gp 1 lb. A holy symbol is a representation of a god or pantheon. It might be an amulet depicting a symbol representing a deity, the same symbol carefully engraved or inlaid as an emblem on a shield, or a tiny box holding a fragment of a sacred relic. A cleric or paladin can use a holy symbol as a spellcasting focus. To use the symbol in this way, the caster must hold it in hand, wear it visibly, or bear it on a shield.
Spellbook 50 gp 3 lb. Essential for wizards, a spellbook is a leather-bound tome with 100 blank vellum pages suitable for recording spells.
