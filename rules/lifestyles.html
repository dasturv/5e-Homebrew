Variant Rule: Lifestyles

In standard 5e DnD, there is not much incentive to take care of yourself. Why sleep in a castle when roughing it in a tent provides the same benefits? The following variant rule adds a bit more complexity to this system.

Inspiration: Shadowrun.

Lifestyles

When using this variant rule you must spend coin in order to maintain your lifestyle. Choose one of the lifestyle levels below. Each lifestyle level has a daily cost you must spend in order to maintain it. At the end of a long rest, you gain the effects of your maintained lifestyle level, provided you have maintained it for at least a week. If you have maintained multiple lifestyles in the past week, you gain the benefit of their average level (rounded down.)

Lifestyles
Level Lifestyle Daily Cost Description Effect
0 Wretched Free You live in inhumane conditions. With no place to call home, you take shelter where you can find it. Poor access to food and clean water leaves you begging for scraps. Disease, violence and hunger are all commonplace for you. Make a DC 20 Constitution saving throw. On a failure you do not recover any hit points during the long rest.
1 Squalid 1 sp You have some shelter, such as a leaky stable, mud floored hut or a vermin infested boarding house. Hunger, disease and violence are still commonplace for you. Make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw. On a failure you can only recover a number of hit points equal to one quarter of your maximum hit points.
2 Poor 5 sp You live in sufficient, if uncomfortable, conditions. With access to simple food and lodging such as a flophouse or a common room above a tavern, you are protected from the elements and starvation, but poor nutrition and disease still impact you regularly. Make a DC 10 Constitution saving throw. On a failure you can only recover a number of hit points equal to one half of your maximum hit points.
3 Modest 1 gp You can ensure you are out of the slums and that you can maintain your equipment. You live in an older part of town renting a room in a boarding house, inn or temple. You get enough food and your living conditions are clean if simple. -
4 Comfortable 5 gp You can afford nicer living conditions, food and clothing. You live in a small cottage in a middle class neighborhood or a private room in an inn. You gain a number of temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
5 Wealthy 10 gp You live in luxury, though not quite to the point of nobility. You have access to high quality food and respectable lodgings such as a spacious home in a good part of town or a suite in a fine inn. You likely have a small staff of servants. You gain a number of temporary hit points equal to twice your proficiency bonus.
6 Aristocratic 25 gp You live a life of plenty and comfort. You never go hungry nor thirsty, and have access to the best food and lodging your community can provide. Large homes, fine dining, skilled tailors, expansive retinues of servants attending your every need. You have access to the richest and most powerful people around, such as politicians, guild leaders, high priests and nobility (and all the treachery that comes with them.) You gain a number of temporary hit points equal to three times your proficiency bonus.
