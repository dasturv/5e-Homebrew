Variant Rule: Traits

Standard DnD 5e's character creation process is incredibly deep... to a point. Compared to many other editions and systems, it is downright simple (as it was designed to be). However, using these rules, a bit more flavor and character can be built into player characters right form the start, granting both strengths and weaknesses to play around with.

Inspiration: OneDnD.

Character Creation

While using this variant rule, players do not choose a background, instead creating one. Each player gains the following benefits upon character creation:

  • You gain proficiency in any two skills of your choice.
  • You gain proficiency with one artisan tool, gaming set or musical instrument of your choice.
  • You learn one language of your choice
  • You gain 25 gp in addition to your starting gold in order to purchase equipment.

Boons and Flaws

When using this variant rules, characters gain a number of traits, small bonuses and penalties that work similarly to feats. Upon character creation a player character has one positive trait from the options below. Additionally, when making a character a player may choose to take a negative trait to be able to take an additional positive trait, up to twice (a total of 5 traits, 3 positive and 2 negative). You can only have one of each trait.

Some traits have prequisites, such as spellcasting, and others have incompatible traits. A player cannot choose a trait when they already have on that is incompatible with it. The DM can always override a player's choice in traits if they believe the trait(s) chosen would not be impactful enough in play.
