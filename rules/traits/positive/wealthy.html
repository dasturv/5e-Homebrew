Home

Traits: Wealthy

Positive trait

Incompatible with the Indebted trait

Whether due to an affluent family, a rich inheritence, canny investments or a successful business, you have a fairly continuous stream of income. Whenever you level up, or when you create your character, you gain bonus gold. The amount of gp you gain is dependent on your level and can be found on the table below. If your campaign is using an alternate level of wealth, use the modified values instead.

Level Bonus gp Impoverished gp Abundant gp
1st-5th 25 gp 15 gp 30 gp
6th-11th 75 gp 30 gp 100 gp
12th-17th 125 gp 75 gp 200 gp
18th-20th 300 gp 125 gp 500 gp

Depending on the events of the story, you may not gain the bonus gold immediately upon level up. the extra gold represents the profits or interest gained while you were away, and you can grab your share whenever you return to the business or to a bank connected to your source of income.

Inheritance

If the Dungeon Master allows it, you may select a single vehicle or home to start with. If you do so, you do not gain gold from this trait until the total gold you would have made with is trait exceeds the price of the chosen vehicle or home. The cost of the chosen vehicle or home should not exceed 1,000 gp. Below is a table with some potential choices, alongside the level this trait begins to provide gold again.

Vehicle or Home Cost Level
Carriage and Draft Horse 150 gp 7th
Large House 1,000 gp 16th

If this vehicle or home is ever destroyed, the player with this trait can attempt to claim insurance on it (if applicable), allowing them to recieve gold equal to half the chosen vehicle or home's value.
