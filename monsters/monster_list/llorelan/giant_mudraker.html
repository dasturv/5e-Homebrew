Giant Mud-Raker

One of the few peaceful creatures within the Dreadmoor, the Giant Mud-Rakers are true behemoths. Covered in a thick carapace and far too big for even the largest of the Dreadmoor's predators, the peaceful Mud-Rakers sift through the substrate of the swamp in search of rotting material. While capable of eating just about anything, they rarely take live prey, preferring to leisurely sift through the plentiful muck of their home. The Giant Mud-Rakers look somewhere between a giant horseshoe crab and a pill bug, with a massive tear drop shaped body covered in thick, overlapping plates of chitin. Their only vulnerable spots exposed normally are their six massive black eyes and (arguably) their mouth, which is covered by a dozen well armored feeding mandibles. Their legs are kept beneath their body, where their far softer underbelly is. Despite thier usual sluggish demeanor, they are capable of sudden bursts of speed.
