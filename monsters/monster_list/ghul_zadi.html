Ghul Zadi, the Voiceless One

The most secretive and mysterious of the Dragonlords, Ghul Zadi dwells deep in the undercity, pulling the strings from the shadows below Valastar. Few have ever heard of Ghul, and even fewer have had direct dealings with the ancient black dragon. Only the highest ranking members of the Ebonfangs, a deadly order of assassins and spies, have ever laid eyes upon the dragonlord themselves, and none dare relate the experience. Ghul remains the secret master of dealing and overcoming death, with Valastars deadliest assassins and most depraved necromancers ultimately answering to it. Ghul does not control a territory beyond its lair unlike the other dragonlords, instead it prowls the undercity and other places where the light fails.

Ghul Zadi
Gargantuan dragon, chaotic evil
Armor Class: 22 (natural armor)
Hit Points: 437 (25d20 + 187)
Speed: 60 ft., burrow 40 ft., climb 60 ft., swim 60 ft.
STR DEX CON INT WIS CHA
27 (+8) 17 (+3) 25 (+7) 19 (+4) 23 (+6) 19 (+4)
Saving Throws: Dex +9, Con +14, Wis +14, Cha +11
Skills: Deception +11, Perception +20, Stealth +17
Damage Resistances: necrotic
Damage Immunities: acid
Senses: blindsight 60 ft., darkvision 240ft., passive Perception 30
Languages: Abyssal, Common, Draconic, Shadowspeech, Undercommon
Challenge: 23 (50,000 XP)
Amphibious: Ghul can breathe air and water.
Legendary Resistance (3/Day): If Ghul fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.
Living Shadow: While in dim light or darkness, Ghul has resistance to damage that isn't force, psychic, or radiant.
Shadow Stealth: While in dim light or darkness, Ghul can take the Hide action as a bonus action.
Sunlight Sensitivity: While in sunlight, Ghul has disadvantage on attack rolls, as well as on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.
Innate Spellcasting: Ghul's spellcasting ability is Charisma (spell save DC 19). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:
At will: Darkness, Invisibility
3/day: Shadow of Moil
Actions
Multiattack.Ghul can use its Frightful Presence. It then makes three attacks: one with its bite and two with its claws.
Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 19 (2d10 + 9) piercing damage plus 9 (2d8) acid damage.
Claw. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 15 (2d6 + 8) slashing damage.
Tail. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 20 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (2d8 + 8) bludgeoning damage.
Wing Talons. Melee Weapon Attack: +15 to hit, reach 20 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (2d8 + 8) slashing damage plus 9 (2d8) acid damage. The target must succeed on a DC 23 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
Frightful Presence. Each creature of Ghul's choice that is within 120 feet of it and aware of it must succeed on a DC 19 Wisdom saving throw or become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. If a creature's saving throw is successful or the effect ends for it, the creature is immune to Ghul's Frightful Presence for the next 24 hours.
Acid Breath (Recharge 5-6). Ghul exhales acid in a 90-foot line that is 10 feet wide. Each creature in that line must make a DC 22 Dexterity saving throw, taking 67 (15d8) acid damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
Legendary Actions

Ghul can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. It regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

Detect. Ghul makes a Wisdom (Perception) check.
Tail Attack. Ghul makes a tail attack.
Wing Talon Attack (Costs 2 actions). Ghul makes a wing talon attack. It then can move up to half its speed.
Cast Spell (Costs 2 actions). Ghul casts one spell. It can only take this action if it has not cast a spell since the start of its last turn.

Ghul Zadi's Lair

The Pit, a vast chute a mile wide that descends from the Warrens deep into the undercity, is the closest thing Ghul Zadi has to a lair. Prowling silently and unseen amongst the piles of refuse cast down from above, swimming through fetid pools and burrowing into mountains of debris, Ghul rules over all that was lost. The few souls who dare to rummage through the waste tell tales of an invisible beast that stalks and kills greedy scavengers, with those who ignore such warnings serving as Ghul's dinner. The few who follow the dragonlord seek its guidance in this realm of decay and death, recieving fell instructions annd dark wisdom from their patron.
