Tactician's Skill

The following maneuvers are available to all scholars, regardless of pursuit.

Administer Aid

On your turn, you can use an action and expend one superiority die to tend to the wounds of one of your companions. When you do so, choose a creature within 5 feet of you. That creature regains hit points equal to the superiority die roll + your Intelligence modifier.

Assess the Situation

When you make an Intelligence(Investigation) or a Wisdom(Perception) check, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the ability check.

Bait and Switch

When you're within 5 feet of a creature on your turn, you can expend one superiority die and switch places with that creature, provided you spend at least 5 feet of movement and the creature is willing and isn't incapacitated. This movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks.

Roll the superiority die. Until the start of your next turn, you or the other creature (your choice) gains a bonus to AC equal to the number rolled.

Exploit Weakness

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to hit the target in a weak spot in it's defenses. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the attack's damage ignores resistances.

Evasive Footwork

When you move, you can expend one superiority die, rolling the die and adding the number rolled to your AC until you stop moving.

One Step Ahead

When you make an initiative roll, you can expend one superiority die and add the die to the roll, provided you aren't incapacitated.

Reasoned Defense

When you make a saving throw against an effect you can see, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die, rolling the die and adding it to the saving throw's total. You can use this maneuver before or after making the saving throw, but before the DM says whether the saving throw succeeds or fails.

Sage Advice

Whenever you take the help action, you can expend one superiority die. When the creature you are helping makes the ability check or attack roll you are helping with, they add your superiority die to the roll's total.

Uncanny Accuracy

When you make a weapon attack roll against a creature, you can expend one superiority die to add it to the roll. You can use this maneuver before or after making the attack roll, but before any effects of the attack are applied.

Undercut Defenses

On your turn, you can use a bonus action and expend one superiority die to point out the weaknesses in a target's defenses. When you do so, choose a creature within 30 feet of you. That creature must subtract the number rolled from the next saving throw it makes before the start of your next turn.

Naturalist Maneuvers

The following maneuvers are only available to scholars who follow the Naturalist pursuit.

Dazing Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to disorient your target. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Constitution saving throw. On a failed save, the target has disadvantage on attack rolls until the end of its next turn.

Deliberate Movement

On your turn, you can use a bonus action and expend one superiority die to move up to your speed plus the superiority die. You ignore difficult terrain for this movement.

Focused Shot

You can expend one superiority die and use a bonus action on your turn to focus on landing your shot, choosing a creature within 90 feet of you as your target. You have advantage on your next ranged attack roll against that creature this turn. If that attack hits, add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Pinning Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to stop your target in its tracks. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and if the target is Large or smaller it must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save, the target has its speed reduced to 0 until the end of its next turn.

Lunging Attack

When you make a melee weapon attack on your turn, you can expend one superiority die to increase your reach for that attack by 5 feet. If you hit, you add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Naturalist's Skill

When you make an Intelligence(Nature), Wisdom(Animal Handling) or a Wisdom(Survival) check, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the ability check.

Skulk

On your turn, you can expend one superiority die to take the hide action as a bonus action. You add the superiority die to the Dexterity(Stealth) check made as part of this action.

Trip Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to knock the target down. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and if the target is Large or smaller, it must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save, you knock the target prone.

Physician Maneuvers

The following maneuvers are only available to scholars who follow the Physician pursuit.

Doctor's Advice

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you is forced to make a saving throw, you can use your reaction to expend one superiority die and add it to the creature's roll.

Efficient Administration

On your turn, you can expend one superiority die to use a Healer's kit or administer a potion of healing as a bonus action. A creature who is stabilized or recovers hit points from this action regains additional hit points equal to the superiority die roll.

Emergency Response

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you takes damage, you can use your reaction and expend a superiority die to move up to half of your speed plus the superiority die, provided you move towards the creature that took damage. This movement does not provoke opportunity attacks.

Hemorrhaging Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to cause massive bleeding in your target. The target must make a Constitution saving throw, taking additional necrotic damage equal to the superiority die + your Intelligence modifier on a failure.

Numbing Concoction

On your turn, you can use a bonus action and expend one superiority die to administer a numbing elixir to an ally. When you do so, choose a friendly creature within 5 feet of you. That creature gains temporary hit points equal to the superiority die roll + your Intelligence modifier.

Physician's Skill

When you make an Dexterity(Sleight of Hand), Wisdom(Insight) or a Wisdom(Medicine) check, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the ability check.

Restorative Care

On your turn, you can use an action and expend a superiority dice to tend to a creature within 5 feet of you. Choose a disease or condition afflicting the creature that has a saving throw to end the effect (examples of such effects include sewer plague, the blindness/deafness spell, and a dragon's frightful presence). That creature can immediately make that saving throw with advantage, adding the superiority die to the total. On a successful saving throw, the chosen effect ends.

Vital Strike

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to hamper your target's ability to recover. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target cannot regain hit points until the start of your next turn.

Politician Maneuvers

The following maneuvers are only available to scholars who follow the Politician pursuit.

Boost Confidence

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you makes an attack roll or ability check, you can use your reaction to expend one superiority die and add it to the creature's roll.

Distracting Strike

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to distract the creature, giving your allies an opening. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll. The next attack roll against the target by an attacker other than you has advantage if the attack is made before the start of your next turn.

Eloquent Quip

On your turn, you can use an action and expend one superiority die to attempt to endear a creature to yourself. Choose a creature who can see and hear you and is not hostile towards you. That creature must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed saving throw, it is charmed by your for one minute or until you or your companions do anything harmful to it.

Goading Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to goad the target into attacking you. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the target has disadvantage on all attack rolls against targets other than you until the end of your next turn.

Menacing Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to frighten the target. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, it is frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

Politician's Skill

When you make an Charisma(Deception), Charisma(Intimidation) or a Charisma(Persuasion) check, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the ability check.

Rallying Words

On your turn, you can use an action and expend one superiority die to bolster the resolve of your companions. When you do so, choose a number of friendly creature who can see or hear you equal to your Intelligence modifier. Those creatures gain temporary hit points equal to the superiority die roll.

Sap Confidence

When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you makes an attack roll or ability check, you can use your reaction to expend one superiority die and subtract it from the creature's roll.

Tactician Maneuvers

The following maneuvers are only available to scholars who follow the Tactician pursuit.

Commander's Strike

When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can forgo one of your attacks and use a bonus action to direct one of your companions to strike. When you do so, choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you and expend one superiority die. That creature can immediately use its reaction to make one weapon attack, adding the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Disarming Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to disarm the target, forcing it to drop one item of your choice that it's holding. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and the target must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save, it drops the object you choose. The object lands at its feet.

Feinting Attack

You can expend one superiority die and use a bonus action on your turn to feint, choosing one creature within 5 feet of you as your target. You have advantage on your next attack roll against that creature this turn. If that attack hits, add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Maneuvering Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to maneuver one of your comrades into a more advantageous position. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and you choose a friendly creature who can see or hear you. That creature can use its reaction to move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks from the target of your attack.

Pushing Attack

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend one superiority die to attempt to drive the target back. You add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll, and if the target is Large or smaller, it must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save, you push the target up to 15 feet away from you.

Scholar's Parry

When another creature damages you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die to reduce the damage by the number you roll on your superiority die + your Intelligence modifier.

Riposte

When a creature misses you with a melee attack, you can use your reaction and expend one superiority die to make a melee weapon attack against the creature. If you hit, you add the superiority die to the attack's damage roll.

Tactician's Skill

When you make an Dexterity(Acrobatics), Intelligence(History) or a Strength(Athletics) check, you can expend one superiority die and add the superiority die to the ability check.
