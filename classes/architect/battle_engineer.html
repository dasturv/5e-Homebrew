Architect: Battle Engineer

The Battle Engineer knows the ever shifting tides of combat requires flexibility and grit. Practical and mechanically minded, a Battle Engineer is an active force on the field.

Bonus Proficiencies

When you choose this framework at 3rd level, you gain proficiency with tinker's tools. If you already have this proficiency, you gain proficiency with one other type of artisan's tools of your choice.

Additionally, your proficiency bonus is now doubled for any ability check you make that uses your proficiency with tinker's tools.

Battle Ready

Also at 3rd level, you become better versed in protecting yourself. You gain proficiency with medium armor.

Flexible Constructions

Beginning at 6th level, you learn to make your constructions easier to modify. Whenever you modify a construction using your Redrawing feature, you may modify one additional construction as part of that same bonus action.

Tactical Detonation

Starting at 10th level, you learn to sacrifice your constructions for explosive power. As an action you can choose one of your constructions, causing it to explode and destroying the construction. The area of the explosion is dictated by how many components the construction has. Each creature within 15 feet (for one component), 10 feet (for two components) or 5 feet (for three or more components) of the construction must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw against your construction save DC. A creature takes 7d6 force damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success.

Once you use this feature, you cannot use it again until you complete a short or long rest.

Battlefield Awareness

By 15th level, you know how to get your constructions up right when you need them. On your first turn of a combat, you may create one construction that you know as a bonus action, using the normal rules for creating constructions.
