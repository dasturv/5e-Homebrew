Architect

An Architect is the master of the battlefield, using magic, technology and intellect to shape environments to their advantage. To the Architect, a battle is an evershifting gameboard, and they bring the tools and knowledge to make their foes play by their rules.

You must have an Intelligence score of 13 or higher in order to multiclass in or out of this class.

Class Features

As an architect you gain the following class features.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d8 per architect level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifer per architect level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: Light armor
Weapons: Simple weapons
Tools: Any two artisan tools
Saving Throws: Constitution, Intelligence
Skills: Choose two from: Athletics, Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Nature and Perception

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) 2 handaxes
  • (a) a scholar's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • any simple weapon
  • a set of leather armor
  • a set of artisan tools of your choice

Alternatively, if you are using starting gold, you start with 3d4x10 gp.

Constructions

Starting at 1st level you gain the ability to create magical objects called constructions. You know two constructions from the Architect Constructions list. You learn additional constructions at certain levels, as show in the Constructions Known column of the Architect table.

Constructions

As an action on your turn you can choose one construction that you know to create, using a set of artisan tools you are proficient with. The rules for placing each construction can be found in its description, and constructions must be created on a solid surface, and is destroyed if the surface it is on is destroyed. A construction has an Armor Class of 12 + your proficiency bonus, has hit points equal to 5 + your architect level, and is immune to psychic and poison damage. Constructions cannot be pushed, pulled or knocked over. If a construction has multiple components, each component tracks its hit points seperately. Unless otherwise stated in its description, a construction lasts for one minute or until destroyed.

Construction Limit

You have a limit to how many constructions you can have active at a time, as shown in the Max Constructions column of the Architect table. If you attempt to create an additional construction while already at this limit, you must choose one of your active constructions to immediately end, or else the creation fails.

Construction Ability

Intelligence is your construction ability. You use your intelligence when setting the saving throw DC for your constructions and when making an attack roll with one.

Construction save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.

Construction attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.

Blueprints

Also at 1st level you learn how to modify your constructions using blueprints. A blueprint modifies the construction it is applied to. You know three blueprints from the Architect Blueprints list. You learn additional blueprints at certain levels, as show in the Blueprints Known column of the Architect table.

Whenever you create a construction, you choose one of your known blueprints to apply to it. Each construction can have only one blueprint applied to it at a time.

Redrawing

Starting at 2nd level, you learn how to adjust your constructions in the heat of battle. As a bonus action you can choose one of your active constructions you can see within 60 feet of you and do one or more of the following:

  • You may teleport the construction up to 15 feet in any direction, provided the end position still fulfills all requirements to place the construction. For example, if you teleport a Sentry construction, it can only be teleported into an unnocupied space within 60 feet of you and on a solid surface. If a construction has multiple components, you can teleport each component up to 15 feet independently.
  • You may replace the construction's applied blueprint with another blueprint that you know.

Surveyor's Eye

Additionally at 2nd level, your keen knowledge on structures allow you nearly supernatural insight. As an action you can become magically aware of all mechanical traps, hidden doors or passages, difficult terrain and unstable or damaged portions of structures within 60 feet of yourself. This knowledge does not grant you insight into how to disable or access any of these mechanisms. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier, and regain all expended uses upon completing a long rest.

Architectural Framework

At 3rd level, you choose a architectural framework, a further specialization of your studies. The framework you choose grants you features at 3rd level and again at 6th, 10th and 15th level.

Smart Targeting

Also at 3rd level, you learn to more finely control your constructions. Whenever one of your constructions would force one or more creatures to make a saving throw, you may choose one of those creatures to automatically succeed on that saving throw.

Ability Score Increase

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Streamlined Construction

By 5th level you become far faster at building your constructions. Whenever you take an action to create a construction, you make instead create two constructions. The constructions do not need to be the same type, nor do they need to have the same blueprint applied to them. You still cannot exceed your maximum number of active constructions.

Protective Geometry

Upon reaching 6th level, you learn how to better use your constructions to defend yourself. You have half cover while within 5 feet of one or more of your constructions.

Repositioning

At 7th level you gain the ability to control the battlefield more directly. As a bonus action you can move a creature you can see within 60 feet of you up to 15 feet in any direction. An unwilling creature can make a Strength saving throw against your construction save DC to prevent this movement. A creature of size huge or larger has advantage on this saving throw.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier, and regain all expended uses when completing a long rest.

Quick Thinking

Also starting at 7th level, you can add your Intelligence modifier to your iniative rolls.

Potent Blueprints

By 9th level your blueprints increase in combat effectiveness. Whenever a creature is effected by one of your blueprints, you may choose to deal 1d6 force damage to that creature.

At 17th level, this damage increases to 2d6.

Improved Protective Geometry

When you reach 13th level your ability to protect using your constructions improve. Allies within 5 feet of one or more of your constructions have half cover.

Fieldcraft

By 14th level your skills in navigating the battlefield are enhanced. Moving through nonmagical difficult terrain costs you no extra movement. Additionally, you have advantage on saving throws to prevent or end effects that reduce your movement speed.

Seamless Workflow

Starting at 18th level, when one of your constructions are completely destroyed, you may use your reaction to create a new construction.

Grand Design

By 20th level your mastery over the battlefield is without peer, granting you the following benefits:

  • You gain a +1 bonus to all saving throws for each of your active constructions within 60 feet of you.
  • You recover all expended uses of your Repositioning feature upon completing a short or long rest.
