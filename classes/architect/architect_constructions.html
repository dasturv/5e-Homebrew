Aegis

You create an aegis in an unoccupied space you can see within 60 feet of you. The aegis projects a 15-foot cone in a direction of your choice. The first time each turn a creature would make an attack roll within this area, it must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the aegis' applied blueprint on a failure. The blueprint is applied before the attack roll.

At 11th level, the aegis' cone increases to 20 feet.

Amplifier

You create an amplifier in an unoccupied space you can see within 60 feet of you. An amplifier has three charges. Whenever one of your other, non-amplifier constructions within 15 feet of the amplifier causes a creature to be effected by its applied blueprint, you may have the amplifier expend one charge to have that creature be effected by the amplifier's applied blueprint as well. When the amplifier expends its last charge, it is destroyed.

At 11th level, the amplifier starts with five charges instead of three.

Anchor

You create an anchor in an unoccupied space you can see within 60 feet of you. The anchor projects a 10-foot square centered on itself. Whenever a creature moves 10 or more feet within this area in a turn, it must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the barrier's applied blueprint on a failure.

At 11th level the size of the anchor's area increases to 15 feet.

Barrier

You create two posts in unoccupied spaces you can see within 60 feet of you and within 15 feet of each other. The posts are connected by a semi-transparent barrier that is 10 feet tall. The barrier provides half cover. The first time each turn a creature passes from one side of the barrier to the other, it must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the barrier's applied blueprint on a failure. If either of the posts are destroyed, the other one is as well, removing the barrier.

At 11th level you can place the posts up to 20 feet apart when creating or reconfiguring them.

Bulwark

You create up to two bulwarks in unoccupied spaces you can see within 60 feet of you, provided each bulwark ends up adjacent to at least one other bulwark. Each bulwark is an opaque, solid wall that is 5x5 feet wide and 10 feet tall. When a creature within 5 feet of the bulwark destroys it with a melee attack, that creature must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the bulwark's applied blueprint on a failure.

At 11th level, you can create up to three bulwarks when you create this construction, instead of two.

Burster

You create a burster in an unoccupied space you can see within 60 feet of you. At the beginning of your next turn, the burster explodes. Each creature within 5 feet of the burster when it explodes must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw against your construction save DC, taking 2d6 force damage and being effected by the emitter's applied blueprint on a failure.

At 11th level the damage dealt by the burster increases to 3d6.

Emitter

You create an emitter in an unoccupied space you can see within 60 feet of you. The emitter projects a 10-foot radius sphere centered on itself. Whenever a creature ends its turn within this area it must succeed on a Constitution saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the emitter's applied blueprint on a failure.

At 11th level the radius of the emitter's area increases to 15 feet.

Gateway

You create two gateways in unnocupied spaces you can see within 60 feet of you and within 15 feet of each other. The first time each turn a creature enters the space of one of the gateways, they may teleport to the space of the other gateway. When a creature uses the gateways in this way, the must succeed a Constution saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the snare's applied blueprint on a failure. If either of the gateways are destroyed, the other one is as well.

At 11th level you can place the gateways up to 30 feet apart when creating or reconfiguring them.

Sentry

You create a sentry in an unoccupied space you can see within 60 feet of you. When you first create the sentry and as an action on your subsequent turns, you can cause the sentry to make a ranged attack roll against a target within 60 feet of the sentry that you can see. On a hit the target takes 1d6 force damage and is effected by the sentry's applied blueprint.

At 11th level the damage dealt by the sentry increases to 2d6.

Snare

You create up to three snares in unnocupied spaces you can see within 60 feet of you. Snares are hidden; spotting a snare requires a successful Wisdom(Perception) check against your construction save DC. When a creature enters a snare's space it must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw against your construction save DC, being effected by the snare's applied blueprint on a failure. The snare is then destroyed, regardless of if the save was successful or not.

At 11th level, you can create up to five snares when you create this construction, instead of three.
