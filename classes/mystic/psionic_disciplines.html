A psionic discipline is a rigid set of mental exercises that allows a mystic to manifest psionic power. Each discipline has a name, description, psychic focus effect and a list of psionic practices associated with it. A psionic practice has additional information in parentheses (see below), followed by an effect that practice creates.

  • Psi Point Cost. In order to use a practice, you must expend a number of psi points equal to its psi point cost. Some practices allow additional psi points to be spent for strnger effects, as denoted by a + sign next to its cost. A practice's description details what effect spend additional psi points does. If a psionic practice's psi point cost exceeds your current psi limit, you cannot use it.
  • Concentration. Whether or not the practice requires concentration, as if you were concentrating on a spell. This is denoted by conc.
  • Duration. The maximum duration the practice can take effect. The duration can be ended early by losing concentration (provided the practice requires concentration), or if you choose to end it.

Alter Form

Immortal Discipline

You have studied the ways your psionics can modify and shape your body, allowing you control over your mass, size, strength and natural recovery.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on saving throws against poison and disease.

Hardened Defenses (1+ psi points). As a reaction when you take damage, you can reduce that damage by 1d6 for each psi point spent on this practice.

Enhanced Leap (2 psi points; 1 min). As an action you modify your density and strength using psionic power. For the duration your jump distance and height are tripled.

Modify Size (3 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you use your psionic powers to increase or decrease your size. For the duration you are enlarged or reduced as if you were under the effects of the Enlarge/Reduce spell, your choice.

Modify Bouyancy (5 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you modify the density of yourself and up to 10 willing creatures you can see within 30 feet of you. For the duration those creatures can walk on liquid surfaces as if under the effects of the Water Walk spell.

Iron Defense (7 psi points; conc., 1 hour). As an action you make your skin as hard as steel. For the duration you have resistance to nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing and slashing damage.

Psionic Recovery (9 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you enhance your natural healing abilities. You immediately regain 4d8+15 hit points, and regain 1 hit point at the start of each of your turns for the duration (10 hit points each minute).

Your severed body members (fingers, legs, tails, and so on), if any, are restored after 2 minutes. If you have the severed part and hold it to the stump, the effect instantaneously causes the limb to knit to the stump.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Celerity

Immortal Discipline

You have practiced the art of using your psionics to enhance your speed and agility.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, your movement speed is increased by 5 feet.

Blinding Strike (1+ psi points). As an action you dart for with blinding speed, striking a foe. You can move up to your speed without provoking opportunity attacks and make a weapon attack. On a hit, the attack deals an addiontal 1d6 force damage for each psi point spent on this practice.

Quicken (2 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you increase your agility. For the duration your speed increases by 10 feet.

Blurred Movement (3 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you begin to percieve time slower. For the duration creatures have disadvantage on attack rolls against you.

Haste (5 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you psionically increase your speed and agility, though at the cost of endurance. For the duration your speed is doubled, you gains a +2 bonus to AC, you have advantage on Dexterity saving throws, and you gain an additional action on each of your turns. That action can be used only to take the Attack (one weapon attack only), Dash, Disengage, Hide, or Use an Object action.

When the effect ends, you canâ€™t move or take actions until after your next turn, as a wave of lethargy sweeps over you.

Freedom of Movement (7 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you psionically ward yourself from effects that would impede you. For the duration you are granted the benefits of the Freedom of Movement spell.

Psychic Speed (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As a bonus action you increase your speed to blinding levels. For the duration your speed is increased by 60 feet, and moving does not provoke opportunity attacks for you. Additionally, when you move within 5 feet of a creature, it takes 5d6 thunder damage from your shockwave. A creature can take this damage only once per turn.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Crown of Despair

Avatar Discipline

You have learned to harvest seeds of despair in a creature's psyche, wracking it with self doubt and inaction.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on saving throws to prevent or end the charmed condition on yourself.

Crowned in Sorrow (1+ psi points). As an action, one creature you can see within 60 feet of you must make a Charisma saving throw. On a failed save it takes 1d8 psychic damage per psi point spent and it cannot take reactions until the start of its next turn. On a success it takes half as much damage.

Succumb to Apathy (2 psi points). As a reaction when you see a creature within 60 feet of you make a saving throw, you can impose disadvantage on that saving throw.

Visions of Despair (3+ psi points). As an action, you force one creature you can see within 60 feet of you to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save it takes 1d6 psychic damage per psi point spent and its speed is reduced to 0 until the end of its next turn. On a success it takes half as much damage.

Embrace of Hopelessness (5 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action, you force up to three creatures you can see within 60 feet of you to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, those creatures are slowed, as if under the effect of the Slow spell. Those creatures can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of their turns, ending the effect on themselves on a success.

Dolorous Mind (7+ psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action, you choose one creature you can see within 60 feet of you. It must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be incapacitated and have a speed of 0 until this effect ends. The creature can repeat this saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. For each psi point above 7 you spend using this practice, you can target one additional creature within range.

Eye of Ennui (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you create an aura of despair in a 30 foot radius sphere centered on point within 120 feet you can see. Each creature that is within the area when you use this practice or start its turn there must make a Charisma saving throw or be overwhelmed by despair for the duration. During this time, the creature cannot have a speed higher than 10 feet and has disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks and saving throws other than Charisma saving throws. If a creature ends its turn outside of the sphere, they can repeat this saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Crown of Serenity

Avatar Discipline

You have learned how to both achieve and radiate peace, calming emotions and lowering tensions.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on saving throws to prevent or end the frightened condition on yourself.

Peaceful Slumber (1+ psi points; 1 min). You instill a peaceful sleep into a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. Roll 3d8, if the targeted creature has current hit points less than or equal to the total, it falls asleep. A target remains asleep for the duration, until it takes damage or until another creature uses an action to shake it awake. You roll an additional 2d8 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond the first.

Serene Grace (2 psi points; 1 min). You create an aura of peace around a creature you can see within 30 feet of you. For the duration, any creature who targets the warded creature with an attack or a harmful spell must first make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the creature must choose a new target or lose the attack or spell. The effect doesn't protect the warded creature from area effects, such as the explosion of a fireball.

The effect ends if the warded creature makes an attack, casts a spell that effects an enemy, or deals damage to another creature.

Calm Emotions (3 psi points; conc., 1 min). You suppress the strong emotions of a group of people. Each humanoid within a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point you can see within 60 feet of you make a Charisma saving throw; a creature can choose to fail this saving throw if it wishes. On a failure, the creature's emotions are suppressed, as if under the effects of the Calm Emotions spell. You choose which effect occurs, as if you cast the spell.

Restorative Slumber (5 psi points; 10 min). You put up to three willing creatures you can see within 30 feet of you into a restful sleep. Those creatures fall unconscious for the duration. The effect ends early if a target takes damage or a creature uses an action to shake the target awake. If a target remains unconscious for the full duration, that target gains the benefits of a short rest and it cannnot be effected by this practice again until it completes a long rest.

Aura of Peace (7 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you begin to radiate protective psionic energy in a 30-foot-radius sphere, centered on you. For the duration friendly creatures within the area have advantage on saving throws against spells, magical effects and psionics.

Additionally, when an affected creature succeeds on a saving throw made against such an effect that allows it to make a saving throw to take only half damage, it takes no damage instead on a success.

Eye of Exhaustion (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you create an aura of lethargy in a 30 foot radius sphere centered on point within 120 feet you can see. Each creature within the area when you use this practice or starts its turn there succeed a Wisdom saving throw or fall unconscious for the duration. A target awakens if it takes damage. Once a target is awoken this way, it is immune to this effect for 24 hours.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Dominate Psyche

Awakened Discipline

You have mastered the psionic art of taking over the minds of others, puppeting them against their will.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you gain a +1 bonus to the saving throw DCs of your psionic talents.

Psionic Disruption (1+ psi points). As an action, one creature you can see within 60 feet of you must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save it takes 1d8 psychic damage per psi point spent and it has disadvantage on the next attack roll it makes before the end of your next turn.

Psychic Command (2+ psi points). As an action you instill a one-word command into the mind of a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or follow the command on its next turn. The target is immune to this effect if it is immune to psychic damage or the command is directly harmful to it. Some typical commands can be found on the Command spell. You can target an additional creature within range for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 2.

Psyche Lash (3+ psi points). As an action you psionically lash the mind of a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must make an Intelligence saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes 1d6 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice, and it canâ€™t take a reaction until the end of its next turn. Moreover, on its next turn, it must choose whether it gets a move, an action, or a bonus action; it gets only one of the three. On a successful save, the target takes half as much damage and suffers none of the other effects.

Antagonize (5+ psi points). You stab a violent command into the mind of a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes 4d4 psychic damage and must immediately use its reaction to make a melee attack against another creature of your choice that you can see. If the target canâ€™t make this attack (for example, because there is no one within its reach or because its reaction is unavailable), the target instead has disadvantage on the next attack roll it makes before the start of your next turn. On a successful save, the target takes half as much damage only. The damage increases by 1d4 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 5.

Greater Command (7 psi points; 30 days). Over the period of one minute, you instill a psychic command into the mind of a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must make a Wisdom saving throw or have the command take root, as if you had cast the Geas spell on it. You cannot issue a suicidal command.

Capture Mind (9 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you attempt to seize complete control over a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be charmed by you for the duration. If you or your allies are fighting the creature, it has advantage on this saving throw.

When you use this practice or as an action on your turn, you can take precise control over a target charmed this way until the end of your next turn. During that time the creature only takes the actions you choose, and doesn't do anything you do not allow it to do. Additionally, you can cause the creature to use its reaction, but this requires you to use your reaction as well.

Each time the target takes damage, it can repeat the Wisdom saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Intellect Fortress

Awakened Discipline

You have practiced mental exercises to mystically protect your body and mind.

Psychic Focus. While focusing on this discipline, you have resistance to psychic damage.

Psychic Feedback (1+ psi points). As a reaction when you are dealt damage to by a creature, you deal 1d8 psychic damage to that creature for each psi point spent on this practice.

Mystical Ward (2 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you raise psychic wards against spiritual threats. While this practice is active you are protected as if you were under the effects of the Protection from Evil and Good spell.

Mental Barrier (3 psi points; 8 hours). As an action you veil your mind using psychic power. For the duration your mind cannot be read nor detected, creatures canâ€™t telepathically communicate with you unless you allow it, and you have advantage on saving throws against any effect that would determine whether you are telling the truth.

Reinforced Psyche (5 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you increase your mental fortitude. For the duration you have advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom and Charisma saving throws.

Psionic Warding (7 psi points; 8 hours). As an action you project an aura of disruptive psionic energy. For the duration you and creatures within 30 feet of you cannot be targeted by any divination magic nor magical or psionic scrying sensors.

Mind Palace (9 psi points; 24 hours). As an action you pull up to ten willing creatures you can see within 60 feet of you into an extradimensional space formed from your psyche. You and each of those creatures vanish and appear in a demiplane of your creation. You can create any sort of floor plan or layout for your mind palace, but it cannot exceed 50 cubes, each being 10 feet on a side. Your mind palace is furnished and decorated as you choose, though any items from the mind palace disappear if removed from the mind palace. At the end of the duration, or as an action, a creature is expelled from the mind palace, appearing in the empty space closest to where you were when you used this practice.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Knowledge Tap

Nomad Discipline

You have studied how to access the noosphere, the realm of all knowledge, in order to enhance your skills and learn new ones.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you gain a +1 bonus to Intelligence checks.

Data Overload (1+ psi points). As an action you overload the mind of a creature you can see within 90 feet of you. That creature must make an Intelligence saving throw. On a failed saving throw it takes 1d8 psychic damage and has disadvantage on ability checks until the end of your next turn. On a successful save it takes half damage and does not have disadvantage.

Psychic Understanding (2 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you tap into the noosphere to understand languages. For the duration you understand the literal meaning of any spoken language that you hear. You also understand any written language that you see, but you must be touching the surface on which the words are written. It takes about 1 minute to read one page of text.

This effect doesnâ€™t decode secret messages in a text or glyph, such as an arcane sigil, that isnâ€™t part of a written language..

Skill Tap (3 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you tap into the noosphere, gaining proficiency in one skill or tool that you lack proficiency for the duration.

Psychic Message (5 psi points). As an action you telepathically send a short message of twenty-five words or less to a creature with which you are familiar. The creature hears the message in its mind, recognizes you as the sender if it knows you, and can answer in a like manner immediately. The spell enables creatures with Intelligence scores of at least 1 to understand the meaning of your message. You can send the message across any distance and even to other planes of existence, but if the target is on a different plane than you, there is a 5 percent chance that the message doesn't arrive.

Skill Enhancement (7 psi points; conc., 1 hour). As an action you use your connection to the noosphere to deepen your understanding of your own talent. You gain expertise in one skill or tool of your choice; until the spell ends, you doubles your proficiency bonus for ability checks you makes that use the chosen skill or tool.

You must choose a skill or tool in which you are proficient and that isnâ€™t already benefiting from an effect, such as Expertise, that doubles your proficiency bonus.

Duplicate Mind (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you create a psionic duplicate of yourself made of pure knowledge. The duplicate looks like you, but is made of flickering runes and text, and appears in a space adjacent to you. The duplicate can take actions and otherwise be affected as a normal creature. It has your statistics except it lacks any magical equipment, has half your maximum hit points, has a number of unspent psi points equal to four times your psi limit, and cannot use the psychic trance feature.

You control the duplicate, and it has its own turn directly after you in initiative order. The duplicate lacks the ability to learn or become more powerful, so it never increases its level or other abilities, nor can it regain expended psi slots.

When the duplicate is reduced to 0 hit points or the duration ends, the duplicate dissipates into intangible runes and vanishes.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Mantle of Joy

Avatar Discipline

You have learned to tap into your joy, radiating out an energizing psychic energy.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks.

Instill Confidence (1+ psi points). As an action, one creature you can see within 60 feet of you gains 1d4 temporary hit points for each psi point spent.

Joyous Presence (2 psi points). As a reaction when you see a creature within 60 feet of you make a saving throw, you can grant advantage on that saving throw.

Aura of Jubilation (3 psi points; 1 min). As an action you begin emitting a distracting field of mirth and celebration. Choose any number of creatures you can see within 30 feet of you. Each of those creatures must make a Wisdom saving throw. If you or your companions are fighting a target, it gets advantage on the save. On a failed save, the target has disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Insight) checks for the duration.

Aura of Hope (5 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you begin radiating pure hope. For the duration, creatures of your choice within 30 feet of you have advantage on Wisdom saving throws and death saving throws, and regains the maximum number of hit points possible from any healing.

Psychic Charm (7+ psi points; 1 hour). As an action, you choose one creature you can see within 30 feet of you. It must make a Wisdom saving throw, and does so with advantage if you or your companions are fighting it. If it fails the saving throw, it is charmed by you for the duration or until you or your companions do anything harmful to it. The charmed creature is friendly to you. When the effect ends, the creature knows it was charmed by you. For each psi point above 7 you spend using this practice, you can target one additional creature within range.

Eye of Euphoria (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you create an aura of absolute joy in a 30 foot radius sphere centered on point within 120 feet you can see. Each creature of your choice within the area is filled with a sense of overwhelming euphoria. For the duration, the creature's speed is increased by 10 feet and has advantage on attack rolls, ability checks and saving throws.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Mantle of Rage

Avatar Discipline

You have learned to weaponize both your rage and the fury of your opponents.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on Strength (Athletics) checks.

Savage Strike (1+ psi points). You channel your rage and hatred into an attack. As an action you make a melee attack. If the attack hits, the attack deals an additional 1d6 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice. Additionally, the target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of you until the end of your next turn.

Draw Ire (2 psi points; conc., 1 min). As a bonus action you instill a fury into the mind of a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become enraged by you for the duration. While enraged in this way, the creature has disadvantage on attack rolls that do not include you as a target. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Instill Violence (3 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you instill violent thoughts into a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be charmed by you for the duration. While charmed this way a creature's face becomes twisted with rage.

The charmed target must use its action before moving on each of its turns to make a melee attack against a creature other than itself that you mentally choose. The target can act normally on its turn if you choose no creature or if none are within its reach.

On your subsequent turns, you must use your action to maintain control over the target, or the effect ends. Also, the target can make a Wisdom saving throw at the end of each of its turns. On a success, the effect ends.

Aura of Fury (5 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you begin radiating an aura of rage. For the duration creatures of your choice within 30 feet of you deal an extra 1d4 damage when it hits with a weapon attack. The extra damage is the same type as the weapon used for the attack.

Psychic Scream (7+ psi points). As an action you unleash a destructive psychic scream, channeling your rage. Each creature within a 60-foot cone originating from you must make a Constitution saving throw. A target takes 8d8 thunder damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success. The damage increases by 1d8 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 7.

Eye of Discord (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you create an aura of fury in a 30 foot radius sphere centered on point within 120 feet you can see. Each creature that is within the area when you use this practice or start its turn there must make a Charisma saving throw or be overwhelmed by rage for the duration. During this time, the creature is incapable of meaningful communication and must use its action on its turn to attack the nearest creature. If there is no targets within its range, it must use its movement to move towards the nearest creature. If a creature ends its turn outside of the sphere, it can repeat this saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Mantle of Terror

Avatar Discipline

You have studied the inner workings of fear, making you adept at spreading panic amongst your foes.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on Charisma (Intimidation) checks.

Spike Adrenaline (1+ psi points). As an action you force one creature you can see within 60 feet of you to make an Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save the target takes 1d8 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice and must immediately use its reaction, if available, to move as far as its speed allows away from you. The creature doesnâ€™t move into obviously dangerous ground, such as a fire or a pit. On a success the creature takes half as much damage and doesn't have to move away.

Frighten (2+ psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you instill the fear of death into the mind of a creature you can see within 60 feet of you. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become frightened of you for the duration. You can target an additional creature within range for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 2.

Phantasms (3+ psi points; 1 min). As an action you overwhelm the mind of a creature you can see within 30 feet of you with terrifying visions. The target must make an Intelligence saving throw. If it fails, the target is either blinded for the duration. At the end of each of its turns, the target can repeat the saving throw. On a success, the effect ends on itself. You can target an additional creature within range for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 3.

Blind Panic (5 psi points; conc., 1 min). You instill an overwhelming panic in the mind of a creature you can see within 90 feet of you. That creature must succeed on an Intelligence saving throw or become incapable of distinguishing friend and foe, as if it was under the effects of the Enemies Abound spell. The creature can repeat the saving throw each time it takes damage, ending the effect on itself on a success. A creature immune to being frightened automatically succeeds on this saving throw.

Deepest Fear (7 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you attack the mind of a creature you can see within 90 feet of you, using its deepest fears. The target must make a Wisdom saving throw. The target takes 7d6 psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success. Also on a failed save, the fear lingers. At the end of each of its turns while the effect lasts, the target repeats the saving throw, taking 3d6 psychic damage on a failed save and ending the effect on itself on a success.

Eye of Terror (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you create an aura of fear in a 30 foot radius sphere centered on point within 120 feet you can see. Each creature that is within the area when you use this practice or start its turn there must make a Wisdom saving throw or be frightened by you for the duration. While frightened this way a target drops whatever it is holding and must move at least 20 feet away from you on each of its turns, if able. If a creature ends its turn outside of the sphere, they can repeat this saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Obfuscate

Nomad Discipline

You have studied the ways of muddling the mind and misleading your foes using your psychic powers.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on Charisma (Deception) checks.

Muddle the Mind (1+ psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you place a distracting psychic field in the minds of a number of creatures equal to the number of psi points spent on this practice that you can see within 30 feet of you. Each target must succeed on a Charisma saving throw or be distracted for the duration. Whenever a distracted creature makes an attack roll or saving throw, it must roll a d4 and subract the number rolled from the attack roll or saving throw.

Psionic Disguise (2 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you change your appearance as if you cast the Disguise Self spell.

Invisibility (3 psi points; conc., 1 hour). As an action you become invisible for the duration. Anything you are wearing or carrying is invisible as long as it is on you. The effect ends if you attack, cast a spell or use a psionic practice.

Major Delusion (5 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you create an image of an object, creature or some other visible phenomenon that is no larger than 20-foot cube, appearing at a spot you can see within 120 feet of you. The image has the same properties as the image created by the Major Image spell.

Delusion Duplicate (7 psi points; conc., 1 hour). As an action you become invisible at the same time that an illusory double of you appears where you are standing. The double lasts for the duration, but the invisibility ends if you attack, cast a spell or use a psionic practice. You can use your action to move your illusory double up to twice your speed and make it gesture, speak, and behave in whatever way you choose.

You can see through its eyes and hear through its ears as if you were located where it is. On each of your turns as a bonus action, you can switch from using its senses to using your own, or back again. While you are using its senses, you are blinded and deafened in regard to your own surroundings.

Vanish (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you turn yourself and up to five willing creatures you can see within 30 feet of you invisible for the duration. Anything the target is wearing or carrying is invisible as long as it is on the targetâ€™s person.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Precognition

Awakened Discipline

You have studied the ability of future sight, using mystical powers and reasoning to predict future events.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you cannot be surprised.

Unerring Darts (1+ psi points). As an action you send out a series of psionic darts to where a creature is going to be. You create a number of darts equal to the number of psi points spent on this practice. Each dart hits a creature of your choice you can see within 90 feet of you. Each dart deals 1d4+2 force damage. The darts all strike simultaneously and you can direct them to hit one creature or several.

Temporal Preparation (2 psi points; 8 hours). Over the period of a minute, you look into the future for points of conflict. For the duration you can add 1d8 to your initiative rolls.

Futuresight (3 psi points). Over a period of one minute you focus on a specific course of action you plan to take within the next 30 minutes. The DM chooses if the action will lead to good results, bad results, both good and bad results, or neither good nor bad results.

Precognitive Defense (5 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you focus on seeing briefly into the future. For the duration, any creature has disadvantage on attack rolls against you, and you have advantage on Dexterity saving throws.

Glimpse the Future (7 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you instill a brief moment of precognition in up to three creatures you can see within 90 feet of you. For the duration, whenever a target makes an attack roll, ability check or saving throw, it can choose to end this effect on themselves to roll an additional d20 and take either result. Alternatively, when an attack roll is made against a target, it can choose to end this effect on itself to roll a d20 and choose which roll to use, the one it rolled or the one the attacker rolled.

If the original d20 roll has advantage or disadvantage, the creature rolls the additional d20 after advantage or disadvantage has been applied to the original roll.

Probability Collapse (9 psi points; 8 hours). As an action you reach your mind into all potential futures, gaining insight. Roll three d20s and record the results. For the duration whenever you see a creature make an attack roll, ability check or saving throw within 90 feet of you, you can use your reaction to replace that roll with one of the d20s you recorded. When you use a d20 this way, it is expended and cannot be used again.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Psionic Arrow

Nomad Discipline

You have learned to infuse ranged combat with your psionic abilities, dealing death from a distance with uncanny accuracy.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls using ranged weapons you are proficient with.

Unerring Accuracy (1+ psi points). When you make an attack roll using a ranged weapon you are proficient with, you can gain a +1 bonus to that roll for each psi point spent on this practice. You can add this bonus before or after the roll, but before you know if the attack hits or misses.

Psychic Thorns (2+ psi points). As an action you make a ranged weapon attack. If the attack hits, the normal effect of the attack occurs, then the target and each creature within 5 feet of it must make an Intelligence saving throw. A creature takes 1d10 psychic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success. The damage increases by 1d10 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 2.

Awakened Arrow (3+ psi points; 8 hours). As an action you instill limited consciousness into up to four pieces of nonmagical ammunition such as arrows or bolts, planting them onto the ground to protect an area. For the duration whenever a creature that isn't you or a creature you designate when you use this practice comes within 30 feet of the ammunition for the first time on a turn or ends its turn there, one piece of ammunition flies up to strike it. the creature must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or take 1d6 piercing damage. The piece of ammunition is then destroyed. The effect ends when there is no ammunition left. You can affect two additional pieces of ammunition for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 3.

Psionic Barrage (5+ psi points). As an action you create a barrage of psionic projectiles in a 60-foot cone. Each creature within that area must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw. A target takes 3d8 force damage on a failure, and half as much damage on a success. The damage increases by 1d8 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 5.

Psychic Quiver (7 psi points; conc., 1 min). As a bonus action you infuse a ranged weapon you are holding with psionic power. Once per turn, either when you use this practice or as a bonus action on subsequent turns, you can make two ranged attacks with the infused weapon. Throughout the duration, the weapon does not require ammunition, creating a magic piece of ammunition that dissapears after it hits or misses a target, and deals force damage instead of its normal damage. If the weapon leaves your hands, the effect ends.

Soul Arrows (9 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you create seven pieces of magical ammunition, such as arrows or bolts, out of pure psionic energy. As a bonus action you can make a ranged attack using a weapon and one piece of this ammunition. On a hit a target takes 4d12 force damage instead of the weapon's usual effects. Whether you hit or miss, the ammunition disappears. The effect ends early if you have no pieces of ammunition remaining.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Psionic Weapon

Immortal Discipline

You have studied to enhance you martial prowess with your psionic powers.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls using melee weapons you are proficient with.

Psychic Strike (1+ psi points). You channel your psionic power into an attack. As an action you make a melee attack. If the attack hits, the attack deals an additional 1d8 force damage for each psi point spent on this practice.

Weapon Recall (2+ psi points). As an action you make a thrown weapon attack using a melee weapon you are holding. The weapon gains the thrown 60/120 property for this attack. On a hit, the normal effects of the attack occurs, and the target takes an additional 1d8 force damage. Regardless of if the attack hits or misses, the weapon immediately returns to your hands, provided you have enough hands open. The extra damage increases by 1d8 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 2.

Infuse Weapon (3+ psi points; 1 hour). As a bonus action you touch a nonmagical weapon and infuse it with psionic power. for the duration the weapon becomes a magic weapon with a +1 bonus to attak and damage rolls. The effect ends if another creature holds the weapon. If you spend 6 or more psi points on this practice, the bonus increases to +2. If you spend 8 or more psi points on this practice, the bonus increases to +3.

Sentient Weapon (5+ psi points; 1 min). As a bonus action you instill a fraction of sentience into a melee weapon you are holding. The weapon begins floating in a space adjacent to you and moves on its own. The weapon becomes intangible to other creatures for the duration. When you use the practice you can make a melee psionic attack against a creature within reach of the weapon. On a hit the target takes the normal damage of the weapon, using your psionic ability modifier instead of Strength or Dexterity.

As a bonus action on your turn you can cause the weapon to move up to 30 feet and repeat the attack against a creature within its reach. When the effect ends, the weapon returns to your hand, or falls in a space adjacent to you if you do not hand enough hands free.

The weapon deals an additional 1d6 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 5.

Weapon Storm (7 psi points). As an action you throw a melee weapon you are holding, causing it to strike your foes. Choose up to five creatures you can see within 60 feet of you. Make a melee psionic attack against each target. On a hit a target takes 6d10 force damage. The weapon then returns to your hand.

Psionic Warrior (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you enter a deep battle trance, enhancing your martial prowess. For the duration you gain the following benefits:

  • You have advantage on attack rolls that you make with melee weapons.
  • When you hit a target with a melee weapon attack, that target takes an extra 2d12 force damage.
  • You make Strength and Constitution saving throws with advantage.
  • You can attack twice, instead of once, when you take the Attack action on your turn. You ignore this benefit if you already have a feature, like Extra Attack, that gives you extra attacks.

Immediately after the effect ends, you must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or suffer one level of exhaustion.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Psychic Assault

Awakened Discipline

You have practiced using your mind itself as a weapon, unleashing powerful aggressive psionic abilities.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with psionic talents.

Psionic Spike (1+ psi points). As an action you force one creature you can see within 90 feet of you to make an Intelligence saving throw, taking 1d10 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice on a failure. On a success the creature takes half as much damage.

Telekinetic Lance (2+ psi points). As an action, make a psionic attack roll against a target you can see within 90 feet of yourself. On a hit that target takes 3d8 force damage. This damage increases by 1d8 for each psi point expended on this practice beyond 2.

Psychic Wave (3+ psi points). As an action, you force creatures within a 30 foot cone (originating from you) to make an Intelligence saving throw. On a failed save the target takes 3d6 psychic damage, plus an additional 2d6 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 3. On a successful save, it takes half as much damage.

Overwhelm the Psyche (5 psi points). As an action you release a debilitating pulse of psychic energy. Choose a 20-foot cube within 90 feet of yourself. Each creature within that area must succeed on an Intelligence saving throw or be stunned until the end of its next turn.

Psionic Blast (7+ psi points). As an action, you force each creature within 30 feet of you to make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save the target takes 1d8 force damage per psi point spent on this discpline and is pushed 30 feet directly away from you and knocked prone. On a successful save, a target takes half as much damage and isnâ€™t pushed or knocked prone.

Psyche Ravage (9 psi points). As an action, you force one creature you can see within 120 feet of you to make an Intelligence saving throw. On a failed save the creature takes 7d12 psychic damage and is stunned for 1 minute. On a success a creature takes half damage and isn't stunned. A stunned creature can make an Intelligence saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Psychic Barrier

Immortal Discipline

You have studied how to manifest barriers of pure psionic energy.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

Psionic Shield (1+ psi points). As a reaction when you are hit by an attack, you conjure a psionic barrier to protect yourself. Until the start of your next turn, you have a +2 bonus to AC for each psi point spent on this practice, including against the triggering attack.

Force Field (2+ psi points; 1 hour). As an action you conjure a field of psionic power around yourself. You gain 5 temporary hit points for the duration. If a creature hits you with a melee attack while you have these hit points, it takes 5 force damage. The temporary hit points and damage increase by 3 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 2.

Psychic Bubble (3 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you create a psionic field in a 10-foot radius sphere that remains centered on you, providing the following effects:

  • The area is difficult terrain to creatures other than you.
  • The attack rolls of ranged weapon and spell attacks have disadvantage if they pass in or out of the area.

Energy Protection (5 psi points; conc., 1 hour). As an action you touch a willing creature, granting it resistance to one type of damage of your choice: acid, cold, fire, lightning or thunder for the duration.

Psychic Wall (7 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you create a wall of psionic energy. You create the wall within 120 feet of you, using the rules from the Wall of Force spell.

Psychic Cage (9 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you create a cage of psionic energy within 100 feet of you, as if you cast the Forcecage spell.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Psychic Inquisition

Awakened Discipline

You have studied the ways of invading the minds of others, prying into secrets and instilling your own thoughts within them.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks.

Psionic Probe (1+ psi points). As an action you violently probe a creatures mind for weaknesses. Choose a creature you can see within 90 feet of you. That creature must make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save the creature takes 1d8 psychic damage for each psi point spent on this practice, and you have advantage on your next attack roll made against that target before the end of your next turn.

Imprint Analysis (2 psi points; conc., 1 min). You focus on an object, attempting to read the psychic residues within it. Choose an object, you must remain within 5 feet of the object for the duration. Once the duration ends you learn some basic facts about it, provided you maintained concentration throughout the duration. You gain a mental image from the object's point of view showing the last creature to hold or touch the object in the last 24 hours. Additionally, you learn of any high-emotion events that occured within 30 feet of the object in the last 24 hours, such as a murder, betrayal or confession of love. You can percieve these events from the object's perspective, though you can only make out vague identifying features of creatures, such as clothing color, verbal tics or size.

Read Thoughts (3 psi points; con., 1 min). As an action you open yourself to the minds of others. For the duration, you can detect the thoughts of other creatures as if you cast the Detect Thoughts spell, except any time the spell would end, this practice ends instead.

Zone of Truth (5 psi points; 10 min). As an action, you create a psychic zone that guards against deception in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on a point of your choice within range. For the duration, a creature that enters the area for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there must make a Charisma saving throw. On a failed save, a creature canâ€™t speak a deliberate lie while in the radius. You know whether each creature succeeds or fails on its saving throw.

An affected creature is aware of the effect and can thus avoid answering questions to which it would normally respond with a lie. Such creatures can be evasive in its answers as long as it remains within the boundaries of the truth.

Psychic Reshaping (7 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you attempt to pull a creature into a stupor and reshape their memories. You do so as if you cast the Modify Memory spell, except you can use your telepathy instead of speaking to the creature.

Psychic Prison (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you attempt to trap a creature you can see within 90 feet of you within a cell only it can percieve. That creature must make an Intelligence saving throw. On a success, the target takes 5d10 psychic damage and the effect ends. On a failed save the target takes 5d10 psychic damage and believes that the area immediately surrounding it is dangerous in some way, such as being surrounded by fire, floating razors or gnashing maws. Regardless of the form the cell takes, the target cannot see anything beyond it and is restrained for the duration. If the target is moved out of the cell, makes a melee attack through it, or reaches any part of its body through it, the target takes 10d10 psychic damage, and the effect ends.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Psychic Step

Nomad Discipline

You have studied ways to move unseen, teleporting and modifying your form to become intangible.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, creatures have disadvantage on opportunity attack rolls targeting you.

Unseen Paths (1+ psi points). As a bonus action you teleport 10 feet for each psi point spent on this practice.

Fade Away (2 psi points). As a reaction when you take damage, you become partially intangible. You gain resistance to the damage of the triggering attack.

Psionic Warp (3 psi points). As an action you attempt to teleport a creature you can see within 90 feet of you. The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (the target can choose to fail) or be teleported to an unoccupied space of your choice within range. The chosen space must be on a surface or in a liquid that can support the target without having to squeeze.

Warp Collapse (5+ psi points). As an action you teleport yourself to an unoccupied space you can see within range. Immediately after you disappear, a collapsing wave of energy manifests, and each creature within 10 feet of the space you left must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save the creature takes 3d10 force damage, and is pulled in a straight line toward the space you were in, ending in an unoccupied space as close to the center as possible (even if that space is in the air). On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn't pulled. The damage increases by 1d10 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 5.

You can bring along objects as long as their weight doesnâ€™t exceed what you can carry. You can also teleport one willing creature of your size or smaller who is carrying gear up to its carrying capacity. The creature must be within 5 feet of you when you cast this spell, and there must be an unoccupied space within 5 feet of your destination space for the creature to appear in; otherwise, the creature is left behind.

Fade Out (7 psi points; conc., 1 min). As an action you become partially intangible. For the duration you are partially translucent and can move through creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. If you end your turn in a creature or object you are pushed back to the last empty space you occupied and take 1d10 force damage for every 5 feet you were pushed.

Plane Shift (9 psi points). As an action you take yourself and up to eight willing creatures who link hands to a different plane of existance, as if you cast the Plane Shift spell. You cannot use this practice to banish an unwilling creature.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Telekinetic Force

Immortal Discipline

You have studied telekinesis, the ability to move objects and creatures using one's psionic powers.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on saving throws to prevent being pushed, pulled or knocked prone.

Psychic Sling (1+ psi points). Choose one object weighing 1 to 5 pounds you can see within 60 feet of you that isnâ€™t being worn or carried. The object flies in a straight line up to 90 feet in a direction you choose before falling to the ground, stopping early if it impacts against a solid surface. If the object would strike a creature, that creature must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, the object strikes the target and stops moving. When the object strikes something, the object and what it strikes each take 1d8 bludgeoning damage for each psi point spent on this practice.

Psionic Cushion (2 psi points; 1 min). As a reaction when you or a creature you can see within 60 feet of you falls, you can slow the falling of up to five willing creatures you can see within range, as if you cast the Feather Fall spell. The effect lasts for a target for the duration or until it touches the ground.

Levitation (3 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you begin to levitate, as if you cast the Levitate spell targeting yourself. The effect lasts for the duration.

Enhance Gravity (5+ psi points). As an action you create a 15-foot-radius sphere of crushing force at a point you can see within 90 feet of you. Each creature in the area must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save the creature takes 4d10 force damage and is knocked prone. On a successful save, the creature takes half as much damage and isn't knocked prone. The damage increases by 1d10 for each psi point spent on this practice beyond 5.

Telekinesis (7 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you awaken your telekinetic abilites, gaining the ability to manipulate creatures and objects as if you cast the Telekinesis spell. The effect lasts for the duration.

Reverse Gravity (9 psi points; conc., 1 min). You channel your telekinetic powers, reversing gravity in a 50-foot-radius, 100-foot high cylinder centered on a point you can see within 100 feet of you. All creatures and objects that arenâ€™t somehow anchored to the ground in the area fall upward and reach the top of the area when you use this practice. A creature can make a Dexterity saving throw to grab onto a fixed object it can reach, thus avoiding the fall.

If some solid object (such as a ceiling) is encountered in this fall, falling objects and creatures strike it just as they would during a normal downward fall. If an object or creature reaches the top of the area without striking anything, it remains there, oscillating slightly, for the duration. At the end of the duration, affected objects and creatures fall back down.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.

Third Eye

Nomad Discipline

You have opened your third eye, granting you perception beyond normal means.

Psychic Focus. While focused on this discipline, you have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks.

Extrasensory Strike (1+ psi points). You attack using your extrasensory abilities. As an action you make a weapon attack. If the attack hits, the attack deals an additional 1d6 force damage for each psi point spent on this practice. Additionally, the target gains no benefit from cover for this attack.

Psychic Alarm (2 psi points; 8 hours). As an action you ward an area within 30 feet of you that is no larger than a 20-foot cube. For the duration, you recieve a mental alarm whenever a tiny or larger creature touches or enters the warded area. When you use this practice you can designate creatures that won't set off the alarm.

The mental alarm alerts you with a ping in your mind if you are within 1 mile of the warded area. This ping awakens you if you are sleeping.

Darkvision (3 psi points; 1 hour). As an action you gain the ability to see in the dark. For the duration you have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet.

Distant Sight (5 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you create an invisible eye within 500 feet of yourself. This practice otherwise works as if you cast the Clairvoyance spell, choosing seeing. You cannot switch between seeing and hearing through the eye.

Psychic Spying (7 psi points; conc., 10 min). As an action you attempt to magically spy on a creature you choose that is on the same plane of existance as you, as if you cast the Scrying spell.

See the Unseen (9 psi points; 24 hours). As a bonus action you gain ability to see things as they actually are. For the duration you have truesight, notice secret doors hidden by magic, and can see into the Ethereal Plane, all out to a range of 120 feet. In addition, you get a +10 bonus to both Wisdom (Perception) checks and your passive perception.

Once you use this practice, you cannot use it again until you complete a long rest.
