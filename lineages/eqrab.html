A Dead God

The Eqrab were born upon the rolling dunes of the Savage Lands to a venomous god known as C'Tar. In ancient times their primordial father clashed with another god: The Great Wyrm, and while his venom eventually slew the great beast, C'tar himself was destroyed nearly entirely in the battle. His skeleton still stands tall as a sun bleached carapace deep within the desert.

Masters of the Desert

The Eqrab are the true masters of the sands. Living nomadically as hunter gatherers, they know the desert like no other. Calling it the Great Mother, they revere the landscape itself as a harsh but nurturing force. They keep in-depth oral histories of seasonal rivers, places where temporary wells can be found, the migration paths of prey animals and ways to procure and prepare tough desert plants for food.

Pragmatic Traditionalists

The Eqrab live in a harsh world where survival is never guaranteed. As such, most would be considered cold at best by the bugs at large, with difficult decisions to leave behind a wounded tribe mate or who gets to eat and who doesn't being common for the Eqrab. As an extension of this, the Eqrab deeply revere wisdom and experience, with elders and storytellers being held in high regard. For an Eqrab clan, having an elder with experiences from lean times can be the difference between holding on to life or a tribe dying out.

Reluctant Outcasts

It is rare for an Eqrab to leave their desert home, with most caring little about what occurs beyond the dunes. Those who do leave are most often exiles or survivors from decimated tribes, and most long to return to the rolling sands of their home. The few who do leave will often find themselves confused in a world alien to them, with vast greenery and even solid ground being confusing at best and disturbing at worst.

Eqrab Names

Eqrab names are most often passed down from generation to generation, with passing elders granting their names to the newer generations in hope of inspiring their survival. Eqrab tribe names are said to have come from the Great Mother, with many taking names representing plants, animals and phenomenom native to their desert home.

Eqrab Names: Abebe, Bonlanle, Chibuzo, Funanya, Isoba, Nneka, Onyeka, Thabo
Eqrab Tribe Names: Brokenbone, Darknight, Dewspine, Dunespider, Eartheater, Lielight, Shiftingsand, Thornypear

Eqrab Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Eqrab share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium. Eqrab tend to be squat and broad, ranging to between 4 and 6 sprigs tall, and weigh up to 350 pebblets.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 sprigs of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of gray.
  • Desert's Intuition. You gain proficiency with one of the following skills of your choice: Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Stealth or Survival.
  • Stinger. You have a stinger that you can use for unarmed strikes. When you hit with your stinger, the strike deals 1 + your Strength modifier piercing damage instead of the normal damage from an unarmed strike and the target must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier.) On a failed save the target takes 1d6 poison damage. This damage increases by 1d6 when you reach certain levels: 5th level (2d6), 11th level (3d6) and 17th level (4d6). You can deal this poison damage only once per turn.
  • Desert Magic. You know the Mold Earth cantrip.
  • Poison Resistance. You have resistance to poison damage and have advantage on saving throws to prevent or end the poisoned condition on yourself.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Eqrab. Eqrab is a language made up of long hisses and made to sound like shifting sands..
