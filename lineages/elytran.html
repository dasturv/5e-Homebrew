Tael watched in awe as Groll strolled forward, chittering a warm welcome as his family surrounded him in excitement.

Diverse and Adaptable

There is no such thing as a typical Elytran. With their widespread populations and large families, no two Elytrans look exactly alike, not even closely related ones. Elytrans range from only 3 sprigs tall to almost 9 and weighing from as little as 30 pebblets to over 300. Their carapaces can be virtually any color, with nearly endless patterns, textures and shapes. Elytrans are also just as diverse in mentality as the are physically, with Elytrans finding their ways into many cultures and lifestyles.

Large Families, Broad Bonds

An Elytran family is a sight to behold. Often over two dozen individuals, their homes tend to be a constant buzz of activity. In addition, families tend to keep in close contact with relatives, even distant ones. It is not uncommon for an Elytran to have at least one family member in every major city (and many minor ones). Elytrans are also well known for adopting other races, and even considering close friends as family.

Love of Differences

Elytrans are almost universally proud of what makes them unique from others. An Elytran with an exceptionally beautiful shell will likely highlight it with jewelry and clothing that accent it’s colors, whereas an Elytran who prides itself on physical strength will go out of its way to prove its strength to others. This love of the unique extends towards others, with many Elytrans prefering companions that are different from themselves. In fact, it is often considered an insult to mimic an Elytran, as it is seen as someone claiming they can do what the Elytran can, but better.

Far and Wide

Elytrans can be found in every corner of Llorelan, and virtually every nation, city and kingdom have their fair share of them. Making their homes wherever they reasonably can, Elytrans cheerfully and tenaciously work their way into many cultures, and while they rarely seek to lead their communities, most settlements have a population of Elytrans far to large to ignore.

Elytran Names

An Elytran family’s method of giving names is as diverse as they are as a race. Many Elytrans have names from whatever culture they were raised from, and many others give themselves a new name throughout their life. When they are left to their own devices, however, Elytran names tend to be short and simple, which is thought to allow the new Elytran to choose their future for themselves without a name to push them down a set path. Family names are rarely used, as with how diverse they can be they tell very little. Instead most Elytrans use a nickname that describes them.

Masculine Names: Bok, Dral, Groll, Jed, Kol, Mard, Thuck, Quil, Ral, Tir, Wult, Yrm
Feminine Names: Bri, Dot, Fyrn, Jas, Kaz, Lis, Nor, Pwyll, Tez, Vex, Yem
Nicknames: Fireheart, Fleetwing, Pearlshell, Rubyeye, Smoothword, Stillsoul, Strongjaw, Quickwit

Elytran Traits

Despite their many differences, all Elytrans share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium or small. You choose when you select this lineage.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 feet. In addition, you have a flying speed of 15 feet or a swimming speed of 30 feet. You choose when you select this lineage.
  • Adaptable. You gain proficiency with one skill of your choice.
  • Hard Shell. Your hard carapace offers you some protection. When you aren't wearing armor, your base AC is 13 + your Dexterity modifier. You can use your hard shell to determine your AC if the armor you wear would leave you with lower AC. A shield's benefits apply as normal when you use your hard shell.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose one of the subrace options below.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and one other language of your choice. Click-clack is the most common language in the world, subsisting of clicks and whistles using the speaker's mandibles.
Longnose

Longnose Elytrans have a strong connection with the natural world and are often empathetic and nurturing.

  • Natural Cure. Whenever you would spend one or more hit dice to regain hit points, you regain bonus hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
  • Skilled Healer. Whenever you would use a healing kit to allow a creature to spend a hit die to recover hit points, that creature regains bonus hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
Spellsoul

Spellsoul Elytrans are those blessed with strange arcane abilities from birth, with innate knacks for a specific element.

  • Spellsoul Affinity. Choose Acrid, Bright, Fetid or Scorching, this is your spellsoul affinity. You gain the 1st level benefits of that affinity, as shown on the table below.
    • Starting at 3rd level and again at 5th level, you also gain the ability to cast a spell using this trait. Using this trait, you can cast that spell once at it's lowest level, and you must complete a long rest before you can cast the spell in this way again. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have.
    • Your spellcasting ability for these spells is Charisma, Intelligence or Wisdom (choose when you select this trait).

Stonehide

Stonehide Elytrans are far tougher than the average bug, often hardworking and tenacious.

  • Durable. Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
  • Natural Weapon. You have some form of natural weapon that you can use for unarmed strikes. The natural weapon can take the form of claws, jaws, or horns. Regardless of the form, when you hit with your natural weapon, the strike deals 1d4 + your Strength modifier bludgeoning, piercing or slashing damage instead of the normal damage from an unarmed strike. You choose which damage type your natural weapon deals when you choose this subrace.
