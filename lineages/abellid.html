Tireless and Graceful

Amongst the bugs of Llorelan, few capture a greater sense of awe than the Abellids. Often slightly taller than an average bug, an Abellid moves with and air of purpose and tirelessness that makes everything they do seem easy. Their carapaces are usually covered in striking yellows and oranges paired with bold black stripes, though reds, browns, blues and even greens are not unheard of. In order to accent their natural beauty, most Abellids wear simple, draping garbs and amber jewelry, allowing a lot of their natural colors to show.

Energetic Artisans

The Abellids are a skilled and active people. Every Abellid has at least one trade they work tirelessly and passionately upon, creating some of the greatest works of artisanship Llorelan has to offer. An Abellid takes great pride in their work, and their mastery of construction, alchemy and especially amber has cemented them as the dominant force on Losterre.

In Service of Others

The Abellids innately crave cooperation and community, working endlessly to expand and protect their sisters, hives and empires. Though, unlike their more insular sisters the Formids, the Abellids eagerly incorporate bugs of other species into their communities, treating them as beloved sisters.

Arboreal Empires

The Abellids live in vast hive-cities in the canopies of mighty trees. Each of these massive cities can house millions of Abellids, each living their lives in service of their hive. These great cities are virtually unassailable, and from their strongholds they project power across Losterre, from the Sevenoire in the far north to the Effroimar in the south.

Extensions of Home

While it is far from rare for a Abellid to temporarily leave their hive to explore or trade, many loathe to be away from home for long. Though sometimes, a rare Abellid is filled with a desire to explore the outside world. when this happens, the Abellids believe that individual experiencing the pull is being called by Evenala to some greater task outside of the safety of the cities, and may even begin their own hive.

Abellid Names

Abellid names tend to both start and end with a vowel and have a flowing, airy energy to them. With the rarity of males, there is no distinction between masculine and feminine names, though specific hives oftentimes have their own naming conventions. In addition to their spoken name, many Abellids also use a unique blend of expressions and body language such as wiggling antennae, buzzing wings, etc. to their name.

Abellid Names: Asalahad, Alavona, Estrili, Ellavali, Ilinitri, Isoliri, Osana, Olarti, Usanti, Uvarosa, Ysildri

Abellid Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Abellids share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium. Abellids tend to be between four and seven sprigs tall, and weigh anywhere between 100 and 300 pebblets.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Artisan. You gain proficiency with one artisan tool of your choice.
  • Busy. You are a buzz of activity, requiring less rest than other bugs. You only need to sleep 4 hours to complete a long rest.
  • Cooperative. You can take the help action as a bonus action. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and regain all spent uses whenever you complete a long rest.
  • Stinger. You have a stinger that you can use for unarmed strikes. When you hit with your stinger, the strike deals 1d4 + your Constitution modifier poison damage instead of the normal damage from an unarmed strike.
  • Wings. Starting at 5th level, you can use a bonus action to magically strengthen your wings for 1 minute. For the duration, you gain a fly speed equal to your walking speed and can hover. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until yout complete a long rest.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose one: Forsor, Judisor or Piquor.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Abellid. Abellid is a language that uses both sound and body language, oftentimes overlapping to create levels of nuance that is hard for other bugs to understand.
Forsor

Natives of Sevenoire, the Forsor are larger and stronger than the rest of their kin. They are masters of construction, creating impossibly strong fortifications rivaled only by the Formid's mountain holds.

  • Powerful Build. You count as one size larger for the purposes of of determing your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag or lift.
  • Reassuring. Whenever you take the help action using your Cooperative feature, you and the creature you help each gain a number of temporary hit points equal to 1d6 plus your proficiency bonus.
Judisor

Residents of Forrevere, the Judisor are the most populous and powerful of the Abellids. Their mastery of amber and beautiful cities are famous across Llorelan.

  • Judisor Magic. You know one cantrip of your choice from the Cleric spell list. Intelligence, Wisdom or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this spell (you choose when you select this lineage.)
  • Lively. Whenever you take the help action using your Cooperative feature, you and the creature you help gain a bonus to their movement speed until the start of your next turn. This bonus is equal to five times your proficiency bonus.
Piquor

Once the Piquor called a great forest home, but the plagues of Maglataz killed their trees and left them bitterly without homes nor amber, eking out a nomadic life upon the Amarre.

  • Nomadic. Your walking speed increases to 35 feet.
  • Spiteful. Whenever you take the help action using your Cooperative feature, the next weapon damage roll you and the creature you help each make before the start of your next turn gains bonus damage equal to your proficiency bonus.
