Empire of Royalty

The fierce Formids live in a strict society, where one's form decides what role they play. From small workers, to massive guards and elegant scions, each Formid has a job to ensure the colony's survival. While other bugs may balk at what they would call tyranny, many Formids take on their duty with pride and vigor. Fiercely loyal to their colony and their queen (whom all Formids are born to), most would gladly give their life for the safety of their home.

Sisters in Strength

All Formids are female besides the rare male scion (called a drone). As such, concepts of gender are vague at best. In addition, since only queens and scions can reproduce, the only family most Formids have is their colony. Formids protect each other as family, a sentiment the rare Formid outside of a colony is often happy to extend to its close friends.

Queen Under the Mountain

The Formids were originally created by their goddess Alowe, the Queen Under the Mountain and twin of the Abellid goddess Evenala. The Formids see themselves as the children and grandchildren of Alowe, and their priestesses and queens go through great lengths to emulate their creator's ability to bring stability and prosperity to their colonies. The mortal form of Alowe is said to be encased deep beneath the earth, impenetrable and safe.

Dedication and Pride

All Formids take deep pride in their work, regardless of what that work is. Workers put great care into their projects, guards pride themselves in their ability to protect others, and scions and queens take great satifaction in growing their colony. Formid architecture and goods are some of the highest quality in all of Llorelan, only rivalled by the Formid's sister race, the Abellids.

Unwilling Adventurers

Very few Formids are willing to leave their colonies. As such the few Formids that do are often envoys from their colony or a survivor from a fallen colony. either way, Formids try and mimic the structure of a colony whenever possible, finding individuals to surround themselves with. The exception are scions and drones, who leave the colony in order to form new ones of their own.

Formid Names

While within their colonies, Formids rarely find the need to have names in the traditional sense. Between themselves the use of unique pheromones is all the identification required. However, while dealing with other cultures, verbal names are required. Formids who spend a lot of time dealing with other races will often take elegant sounding names with lengthy titles. Those who are away from a colony due to happenstance, however, will often pick up a name to do with their appearance or profession.

Formid Names: Aladula, Darutha, Esendyl, Hollista, Illivar, Lyssanti, Oryen, Seleste, Volundi
Formid Nicknames: Big, Cutter, Forager, Hunter, Rocky, Snipper, Tiny

Formid Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Formids share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 sprigs of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of gray.
  • Busy. You are a buzz of activity, requiring less rest than other bugs. You only need to sleep 4 hours to complete a long rest.
  • Cooperative. You can take the help action as a bonus action. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and regain all spent uses whenever you complete a long rest.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose one: Scion, Soldier or Worker.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Formid. Formid is primarily a non-verbal language, relying on pheromones and body language to convey meaning.

Soldier

The largest and toughest of the Formicoid morphs, soldiers protect the colony and fight its enemies. They swarm over the battlefield, working together to bring down the mightiest of foes.

  • Size. Your size is medium. Soldier Formids range from five to eight sprigs, and weight between 150 and 450 pebblets.
  • Powerful Build. You count as one size larger for the purposes of determing your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag or lift.
  • Pack Tactics. You have advantage on an attack roll against a creature if at least one of your allies is within 5 feet of the creature and the ally isn't incapacitated.
Worker

Smaller and more numerous than the other morphs, workers make up the backbone of the colonies. Most workers are artisans, foragers, hunters and farmers, tirelessly laboring to keep the colony functioning.

  • Size. Your size is small. Worker Formids range from two to four sprigs, and weight between 25 and 100 pebblets.
  • Communal. You are considered to be one lifestyle higher for the purposes of lifestyle effects, to a maximum of modest.
  • Oversized Strength. You count as two sizes larger for the purposes of determing your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag or lift. Additionally, you do not suffer the usual penalty for using heavy weapons while small.
  • Specialized. You gain proficiency with one set of artisan tools or one skill of your choice.
