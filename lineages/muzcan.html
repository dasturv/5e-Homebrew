Foul Spawning

The Muzcan are a relatively new race of bugfolk, being created by the fell god Maglataz. Their very nature is shaped by the foul diety's nature, and it is believed that each form the Muzcans take represents a specific aspect of their creator. Each and every Muzcan has some combination of respect and fear for their god, as he is a fickle being who will just as quickly eat his worshippers as reward them.

Cruel Castes

Muzcan culture is completely defined by the form a Muzcan takes upon birth. The first caste is made up of the Amedruzcan (Bloated Muzcans), the foul nobility and priests of their society, being the largest and strongest of their kin. In the second caste there are the Vorazcan (Hungering Muscans), fast and aggressive Muzcans who serve their god as warriors and assassins. The third caste are simply known as Muzcans (or Pitiful Muzcans). Tiny but plentiful, they are seen as barely more than animals by the other castes.

Slaves and Sacrifices

Muzcan living in their corrupted culture view all other species, sentient or not, as potential slaves or feed for their ever ravenous god. They seek out slaves aggressively, especially the Pitiful Muzcans, as they know if Maglataz is not fed, it will be them who are used to sate his appetite instead. Life as a slave to the Muzcan is brutal, cruel and short, always ending with being thrown into the gaping maw of their god.

Spreading Pestilence

The Muzcan race is found in large quantities only within the confines of the Dreadmoor, the twisted remains of what once to be a grand jungle, corrupted and fed upon by Maglataz during his ascension to godhood. The legions of the Dawnkeeper Order keep the Muzcan from breaking out of their stronghold and spreading their god's influence further, though individuals and small groups do occasionally sneak past to wreak havoc in the world at large.

Muscavn Names

A Muscan's name is highly dependent on their status and form. Amedruzcan and Vorazcan tend to have guttural names, with more important Muzcans boasting longer and more complex names. Pitiful Muzcans, if given a name at all, often have demeaning names that show their place in society.

Greater Names: Bolutuz, Doguz, Gorgut, Jhokuz, Korebut, Mokuz, Rogumat, Worzak
Pitiful Names: Burden, Corpse, Fodder, Lacking, Meat, Nothing, Serf, Worthless

Muzcan Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Muzcan share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 sprigs of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of gray.
  • Foul Ancestry. You have advantage on saving throws against poisons and disease.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose either Vorazcan or Muzcan.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Muzcavan. Muzcavan is a grating language with rough consonants and punctuated with harsh buzzing.
Vorazcan

The Vorazcans make up the second caste of their society, and emulate Maglataz's endless appetite. They are quick, lean and starved creatures with a long stabbing proboscis. They attack and drain the life giving fluids of creatures they think they can take, gaining power as they feed on others.

  • Size. Your size is medium. Hungering Muscans tend to be between four and seven sprigs tall, but their lean frames mean they rarely exceed 200 pebblets.
  • Aggressive. You can take the dash action as a bonus action, provided you end closer to a hostile creature than you started.
  • Proboscis. Your sharp proboscis is a natural weapon, which counts as a simple melee weapon with which you are proficient. You add your Constitution modifier, instead of your Strength modifier, to the attack and damage rolls when you attack with this proboscis. It deals 1d4 piercing damage on a hit. While you are missing half or more of your hit points, you have advantage on attack rolls you make with this bite.
    • When you attack with this proboscis and hit a creature that isn't a Construct or Undead, you can empower yourself in one of the following ways of your choice:
      • You regain hit points equal to the piercing damage dealt by the proboscis.
      • You gain a bonus to the next ability check or attack roll you make; the bonus equals the piercing damage dealt by the proboscis.
    • You can empower youself with this proboscis a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.
Muzcan

The pitiful Muzcans are the least of their kind, making up the third and lowest caste in their society, barely above the slaves and beasts according to their larger kin. The pitiful Muzcans represent their creator's virulence, reproducing quickly and spreading wide. Small and cowardly, they live harsh and often short lives.
