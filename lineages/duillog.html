Ancient Devotees

The Duillog do not claim to be descended from any diety. Instead, they pay homage to the Gashalag, the embodiment of the natural order and the entire world. According the Duillog, all life comes from this primordial force, even the gods. The Duillog carefully watch the world around them for signs and messages from the Gashalag, interpreting its will in order to be its hands in the realm of mortals. The Duillog are considered to be some of the first druids and rangers in the world of Llorelan.

Hidden Lords

In ancient times, it was the Duillog who ruled the great forests of the Westlands. When the Vespids rose to power, claiming the great trees for themsleves, there was no conflict. The Duillog merely slipped away, disappearing into the foliage of the forest. In modern times the Duillog live in small, secret societies, still tending to their forests and protecting their sacred sites from the shadows. Despite the power they hold, they have never attempted to remove the other bugfolk from the forest, preferring peaceful coexistence.

Masters of Beast and Leaf

The Duillog all have an innate connection to the natural world, to a degree other races must use magic to emulate. Each and every Duillog have the ability to speak to beasts and plants, as well as turn invisible at will. They tend to large collections of wild bugs, using them as both guards and spies against their bugfolk neighbors. The Duillog know of every beast, plant and stone in their territory, and know exactly how each and every one can aid them.

Holy Pilgrims

The vast majority of Duillog never leave the communities they are born into, much less their forests. Rarely, however, a Duillog is sent out on a mission dictated by the tribe's leaders, often due to interpretations of the Gashalag's will. Such Duillog set out with the understanding that they are to right some wrong or restore balance to the natural order, though few start their journeys knowing what the imbalance is, or even if it has happened yet.

Duillog Names

Duillog names are multi-segmented, with two individual parts. Each half of a Duillog's name is shared with one parent or relative on that parent's side, and is chosen to represent the passing on of traits from ancestors to descendents. Duillog name segments tend to be short, with only one or two syllables

Blattid Names: Asa'Dul, Beelo'Ruk, Folo'Zal, Gor'Sulo, Nes'Tulo, San'Gala, Tor'Bulo, Uta'Dal

Duillog Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Duillog share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium. Duillog tend to be tall, often standing between five and eight sprigs tall, and weigh up to 300 pebblets.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Hidden Step. As a bonus action, you can magically turn invisible until the start of your next turn or until you attack, make a damage roll, or force someone to make a saving throw. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you complete a long rest.
  • Nature's Intuition. You gain proficiency with one of the following skills of your choice: Animal Handling, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Stealth or Survival.
  • Speech of Beast and Leaf. You have the ability to communicate in a limited manner with Beasts, Plants, and vegetation. They can understand the meaning of your words, though you have no special ability to understand them in return. You have advantage on all Charisma checks you make to influence them.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose either Branch or Leaf.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Muscan. Muscan is a grating language with rough consonants and punctuated with harsh buzzing.
Branch

Branch Duillog are lean and fierce bugfolk with carapaces that look like wood and twigs, and some even bear thorns. They have an innate connection with beasts, and are usually especially fond of predatory bugs.

  • Bestial Magic. You can cast the Animal Friendship and Hunter's Mark spells with this trait. Once you cast either of these spells with this trait, you can’t cast that spell with it again until you finish a long rest. You can also cast these spells using any spell slots you have.
    • Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this lineage).
Leaf

Leaf Duillog are graceful and peaceful, with carapaces that are covered in flaps and protrusions that make them appear as if they were covered in leaves and foliage. They have an innate connection with plants, and especially the great trees.

  • Verdant Magic. You can cast the Entangle and Detect Magic spells with this trait. Once you cast either of these spells with this trait, you can’t cast that spell with it again until you finish a long rest. You can also cast these spells using any spell slots you have.
    • Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this lineage).
