Kamakri (Mantises)

Kamakri Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Kamakri share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium. Kamakri tend to be between 5 and 7 sprigs tall, and weigh up to 250 pebblets.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Claws. You sport a pair of long, sharp blades on the end of each arm. When you hit with your claws, the attack deals 1d6 + your Strength or Dexterity modifier piercing damage instead of the normal damage from an unarmed strike.
  • Ambusher. You have advantage on initiative rolls.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose either Blossom or Emerald.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Kamakrin. Kamakrin is an aggressive language filled with quick, harsh sounds.
Ran-Kamakri (Blossom)

Blossom Kamakri are descended from the warrior-monk followers of the demigod Tamotsu, who found tranquility in the valley of blossoms and with the Great Tree of Peace, Kishima, a powerful goddess. From their serene home the Ran-Kamakri seek enlightenment through meditation and training.

  • Calm Mind. You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the charmed condition on yourself.
  • Enlightened Defense. Whenever you fail a saving throw, you can roll a d8 and add it to the total, potentially turning the failure into a success. Once you use this feature to succeed on a saving throw, you cannot use it again until you comlpete a long rest.
Ken-Kamakri (Sword)

Ken-Kamakri wander the Eastlands from coast to coast as warlords, wandering warriors and loyal bodyguards. Driven by a fierce martial pride and sense of honor, they seek battle to further hone their skills in warfare.

  • Brave. You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the frightened condition on yourself.
  • Unerring Accuracy. Whenever you miss an attack, you can roll a d8 and add it to the attack roll, potentially turning the miss into a hit. Once you use this feature to hit an attack, you cannot use it again until you comlpete a long rest.
