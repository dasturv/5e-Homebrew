The music gently drifted between the trees to Darutha and her group. She looked around, but the forest prevented her from pinpointed the songs direction. She began to panic, but then a feeling of lethargy overtook her. One by one her party began to lie down in the moss, their quiet snores barely noticed by her as she slumped to the forest floor. As her eyes began to close, she could barely make out a lone figure dropping from a branch above, watching her...

Enchanting Heritage

The Amhrani come not from a god, but from the pervasive magics found in the north of the Eastlands. As with all the natives of these enchanted lands, they bear unique gifts and powers. The Amhrani are masters of music and enchantment, using their powers to spread joy and wonder wherever they go. From the shores of the Addlebrook to the depths of the Whistlewood, the songs of the Amhrani soothe travellers and delight guests.

Masters of Music

Ever Amhrani is born with an innate love of music, helped by their naturally occuring instrument on their legs called a cricketfiddle. They are widely considered the best musicians in Llorelan, with nobles of other races eagerly competing to have an Amhrani as an entertainer in their court. Their music can soothe and entrance the listener, and the Amhrani are keenly aware of how they can use their powers to influence those around them.

Sacred Song

The Amhrani hold one place sacred above all others: the Singing Stones. Nestled in the arms of the Addlebrook, the Singing Stones are a series of obelisks filled with holes and channels that whistle as the wind blows through them. Every Amhrani visits the singing stones at least once in their lives as a coming of age ceremony, and it is believed that the songs of the stones are what grant them their unique powers, an idea supported by the existance of the Doraithe, Amhrani who never visited the Singing Stones and can no longer produce music and joy.

Wanderers and Nomads

There are few Amhrani who prefer to stay in any one place for long. They form clans and caravans, wandering from place to place in enchanting bliss. Many Amhrani spend at least a portion of their lives away from their kind, seeking out adventure and inspiration for new stories and songs to bring back to their people. As such, every community has hundreds of songs, poems and stories from adventures long past, whether they be herioc, comedic or serve as a dire warning.

Amhrani Names

Amhrani names tend to have an air of whimsy to them, with an emphasis of names that are simply fun to say and hear. The exception to this are the Doraithe, whose names tend to be harsh and aggressive.

Amhrani Names: Addilix, Doridad, Fennelin, Humphred, Ladifen, Porfas, Todilin, Zadulax
Doraithe Names: Borgugog, Gornek, Killegon, Morke, Rodogul, Ugudol, Wurgok, Yndrigel

Amhrani Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Amhrani share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium or small. You choose when you select this lineage. Amhrani range from three sprigs tall to almost seven, and weight between 30 and 250 pebblets.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 sprigs of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of gray.
  • Fey Ancestry. You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the charmed condition on yourself.
  • Mighty Leap. As a bonus action, you can jump a number of feet equal to five times your proficiency bonus, without provoking opportunity attacks. You can use this trait only if your speed is greater than 0. You can use it a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you complete a long rest.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose one: Banished, Meadow or Twilight.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Amhrani. Amhrani is a rythmic, almost song-like language. Though, when a Doraithe speaks it, it tends to sound monotone and droning.
Cuinin (Twilight)

The Cuinin Amhrani are natives to the Whistlewood, and their gentle songs are often heard drifting through its shaded groves and paths. Considered to be the best musicians even amongst their kin, the Cuinin use their powers to tug on the very souls of their listeners, lulling them into sleep and rest.

  • Soothing Magic. You know the Dancing Lights cantrip. Once you reach 3rd level you can cast Sleep. Once you reach 5th level, you can also cast Calm Emotions. Once you cast either spell with this trait, you cannot cast that spell with it again until you finish a long rest; however, you can cast the spell using any Spell Slots you have of the appropriate level.
    • Intelligence, Wisdom or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for the spells you cast with this trait. You choose which ability when you choose this lineage.
  • Cricketfiddle. Your legs have a patch of ruffled carapace that you can use to make music. Your legs count as a musical instrument that you are proficient with.
  • Musician. You have proficiency with the Performance skill.
Doraithe (Banished)

Descended from murderers and thieves exiled by the other Amhrani, the banished now reside in the Savage Lands. Their exile from their homeland has severed their ties to its magic, and as such are cursed to never again make music or bring joy to the world. Now the banished form raiding bands whose joyless buzzing brings only madness and fear.

  • Accursed Magic. You know the Vicious Mockery cantrip. Once you reach 3rd level you can cast Cause Fear. Once you reach 5th level, you can also cast Crown of Madness. Once you cast either spell with this trait, you cannot cast that spell with it again until you finish a long rest; however, you can cast the spell using any Spell Slots you have of the appropriate level.
    • Intelligence, Wisdom or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for the spells you cast with this trait. You choose which ability when you choose this lineage.
  • Song-Severed. You cannot produce music. You cannot gain proficiency with musical instruments, nor can you make Charisma(Performance) checks to play or sing music.
  • Barbed Legs. Your legs are covered in sharp spines that you can use for unarmed strikes. When you hit with your legs, the strike deals 1d6 + your Strength modifier piercing damage instead of the normal damage from an unarmed strike.
  • Unsettling. You have proficiency with the Intimidation skill.
Redeemed Doraithe

Some rare Doraithe have earned the respect and forgiveness of the rest of the Amhrani, granting them special rights to visit the Singing Stones. While some Doraithe achieve this on their own, the most famous group of the redeemed Doraithe are the followers of Iddilig Radani, a group of banished Amhrani that has settled peacefully north of the Savage Lands. Such a Doraithe loses the song-severed trait, as the magics of the Singing Stones is no longer barred from them.

Gealin (Meadow)

The Gealin Amhrani are cheerful nomads, traveling across the Meadowlands in large wagon trains and playing music everywhere they go. Masters of charm and entertainment, they spread glee wherever they go.

  • Cheerful Magic. You know the Friends cantrip. Once you reach 3rd level you can cast Charm Person. Once you reach 5th level, you can also cast Enthrall. Once you cast either spell with this trait, you cannot cast that spell with it again until you finish a long rest; however, you can cast the spell using any Spell Slots you have of the appropriate level.
    • Intelligence, Wisdom or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for the spells you cast with this trait. You choose which ability when you choose this lineage.
  • Cricketfiddle. Your legs have a patch of ruffled carapace that you can use to make music. Your legs count as a musical instrument that you are proficient with.
  • Musician. You have proficiency with the Performance skill.
