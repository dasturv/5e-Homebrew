Miadoir (Termites)

Miadoir Traits

Regardless of what form they take, all Miadoirans share the following traits.

  • Ability Score Increase. When determining your character's ability scores, increase one score by 2 and increase a different score by 1, or increase three different scores by 1. You can't raise any of your scores above 20.
  • Creature Type. You are an insectoid.
  • Size. Your size is medium or small. You choose when you select this lineage. Miadoirans range from three to seven sprigs, and weight between 50 and 350 pebblets.
  • Speed. Your walking speed is 30 sprigs.
  • Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 60 sprigs of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of gray.
  • Fey Ancestry. You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the charmed condition on yourself.
  • Sonic Resistance. You have resistance to thunder damage.
  • Subrace. You choose a subrace and gain its features. Choose one: Marauder, Scorned or Scout.
  • Languages. Your character can speak, read and write Click-clack and Miadoir. Miadoir is a rapid, rhythmic language made up of lightning fast clicks of different tones and body language.
Marauder

The remnants of the former soldier caste, the Marauder Miadoirans form loose bands of warriors, raiding other bugs in terrifying surprise attacks under the cover of darkness, using shock tactics and intimidation to end the battle quickly.

  • Bite. You have a set of bladed mandibles that you can use to make unarmed strikes. When you hit with it, the strike deals 1d6 + your Strength modifier slashing damage, instead of the bludgeoning damage normal for an unarmed strike.
  • Rampage. When you score a critical hit or reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a melee attack on your turn, you can take a bonus action to move up to half your speed and make a bite attack.
  • Savage Attacks. When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, you can roll the weapon's damage dice one additional time and add it to the extra damage of the critical hit.
Scorned

Once the royalty and nobles of the Miadoir, the Scorned now use their innate magic to hamper enemy mages, gathering bands of other Miadoir to attack their hated Scraedai rivals.

  • Spell-Scorn. You know the Chill Touch cantrip. Once you reach 3rd level you can cast Disrupt Spell. Once you reach 5th level, you can also cast Silence. Once you cast either spell with this trait, you cannot cast that spell with it again until you finish a long rest; however, you can cast the spell using any Spell Slots you have of the appropriate level.
    • Intelligence, Wisdom or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for the spells you cast with this trait. You choose which ability when you choose this lineage.
  • Wings. Starting at 5th level, you can use a bonus action to magically strengthen your wings for 1 minute. For the duration, you gain a fly speed equal to your walking speed and can hover. Once you use this trait, you can't do so again until yout complete a long rest.
Scout

Formerly serving as the foragers, artisans and hunters of the Miadoir, now the Scouts live as stealthy raiders, spying on their foes and picking off exposed enemies.

  • Skulk's Cunning. You gain proficiency with two of the following skills of your choice: Animal Handling, Nature, Perception, Sleight of Hand, Stealth or Survival.
  • Surprise Attack. If you surprise a creature and hit it with an attack on your first turn in combat, the attack deals an extra 2d6 damage to it. You can use this trait only once per combat.
